Findings worth noting

  • 5/5 sites have llms.txt — agent-readability is now table-stakes for AI-native B2B.
  • 0/5 block GPTBot in robots.txt — they want LLM training/citation traffic.
  • Rendering split: 3 SSR (Attio, Portkey, Rocketlane) · 2 SPA (CodeRabbit, Lindy). Hero content readable to scrapers in 3/5 cases.
  • Voice mix: 2 corporate · 2 authoritative · 1 playful. Zero founder-voice in this sample.
  • Webflow rate: 2/5 (Lindy, Rocketlane). Higher than expected for a "tech-savvy" cohort.
  • Hero copy bucket: 5/5 benefit-first. Strong signal that the category has converged on outcome-led headlines.

Pipeline architecture

  1. Extractor (Playwright) — 5 pages per site, headers, screenshots, robots/llms.txt probe. NO LLM.
  2. Deterministic (pure Python) — fills ~108 of 148 fields from DOM, CSS, response headers, JSON-LD.
  3. LLM judge (one Haiku 4.5 call per site) — fills ~40 subjective fields with hero screenshot + compact text excerpts.

Per-site cost: ~$0.006. All 50 sites projected at ~$0.30 / ~25 min wall clock.
