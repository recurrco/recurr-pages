MOO App v2 — Membership, Multi-Kitchen, Nutrition

A thought exercise about what the MOO consumer app could look like if it ever became an App Store product. Working title for a possible future milestone — not on any FY27 plan.

Heads up — future vision only

Nothing in this doc is on any current roadmap. No engineering work is committed against it, no dates are real, no team is assigned. This is a long-horizon north star meant to provoke conversation — far from actionable. The current app at moo.kitchen remains the only thing being built and shipped.

Owner: Pratik Drafted 2026-05-01 Source: ~/recurrco/brain/projects/moo/moo-app-v2-vision.md

The current app (moo.kitchen) is a partner-led ordering tool: a customer arrives via a partner QR or invite, adopts that partner as their address, and orders from that menu. It's a strong v1 — Google auth, Razorpay, Slack-bridged chat, and a live order tracker that pulls Outpost fulfilment status — but its identity is "the partner's ordering app that happens to be MOO." The brand is the substrate, not the surface.

v2 inverts that. The app becomes the brand. A customer can install MOO from the App Store, set up a profile, and land in a place where they can engage with MOO without yet having a menu. Menu access becomes earned — either by being near a public MOO kitchen or by being invited into a partner kitchen. Once in, they get the full ordering surface plus a nutrition layer that turns each order into a logged event the customer can actually use.

This doc is the spec for that milestone — the user journey, the delta from today, and the strategic call to make first.

The premise

"I open the app, set up my profile, and land in a place where I do not have any menu accessible to me. I will need to either choose my location or be invited by someone to their kitchen to actually be able to access a menu. Without any menu I am just sitting there doing nothing — I can read about MOO, see coming events, and the latest Instagram story. The moment some kitchen invites me, I get access." — Pratik, Apr 30 2026

Three things change about how the app behaves:

  1. Distribution. App Store + Play Store, not just moo.kitchen PWA.
  2. Identity. Customer-first ("I'm a MOO member") instead of partner-first ("I'm ordering from Cult Fit"). Multi-membership baked in — one customer can be in their employer's kitchen, their gym's kitchen, and the public MOO HSR kitchen at the same time.
  3. Soft landing. A "no kitchen yet" state that's not a dead end — it's where the brand lives. About MOO, events, IG, find-a-kitchen.

Stage by stage

1. Onboarding (3 screens, ~30s). Sign in (Google) → "What brings you here?" (diet, goal, allergens — the fields already on the customers table) → "Where do you order from?" (location permission OR "I have an invite" path).

2. Lobby — no kitchen yet. Soft landing. Greeting, profile chip, four cards:

  • About MOO — story, three cloud kitchens, philosophy, the brand pyramid surfaced as content.
  • Coming up — upcoming drops, retail launches, events. Driven by a new events table on moo-kitchen DB.
  • Latest from Instagram — the existing Behold.so feed (feeds.behold.so/HwwHIa1EJOgp95CAXCb2) already rendering on ministryofoats.com.
  • Find a kitchen — two paths from one card.

A small footer line: "Ordering today? Find us on Swiggy or Zomato." deep-links to the aggregator listings. We don't pretend they don't exist; we just don't lead with them.

3. Find a kitchen.

  • Geo path — tap "Find a kitchen near me" → permission prompt → list of MOO kitchens within ~5 km (the three cloud kitchens are always public-discoverable; partner kitchens appear if they have walk-in enabled AND the customer is nearby).
  • Invite path — enter a 6-character code or scan a QR → "You've been invited to Cult Fit's MOO kitchen by Pratik" → join.

Both paths terminate in kitchen membership. A customer can hold many memberships at once.

4. Member home — kitchen joined. The current /home evolves into a richer dashboard:

  • Greeting + active slot ("Delivering by 9 AM @ Cult Fit Whitefield").
  • Stack of joined kitchens, switchable.
  • This-week nutrition strip ("4 meals · 1,840 kcal · 95 g protein").
  • Coming events @ your kitchens (e.g. an HSR pop-up, a sampling at Cult Fit).
  • Big ORDER NOW CTA → /menu.

5. Menu, tray, cart, pay. Same as today. The customer ordering flow is the strongest part of v1 and doesn't need to change to ship v2.

6. Order tracker. Same as today, plus a one-line nutrition log ("420 kcal, 15 g protein logged for today").

7. Nutrition tab. The new fourth tab. Daily / weekly view of consumption against the profile's health goal, derived from order_items × Outpost recipe-CMS nutrition. Streak counter. "What's missing today?" — menu suggestions filtered by the day's protein / fibre / kcal gap.

The strategic call

There are two interpretations of "kitchen invitation":

  1. Strict invite-only. Every kitchen requires a partner-issued code. A walk-in customer in HSR cannot see the MOO HSR menu without an invite.
  2. Public + private kitchens. MOO-owned cloud kitchens (HSR, Domlur, Whitefield) are public-discoverable. Partner kitchens (Cult Fit @ X, employer cafeterias, gyms) require invite.

Recommendation — option 2

Public + private maps cleanly onto what's already true:

  • Direct + walk-in margins are 36 % vs aggregator's 25 %. The MOO brand app should own that direct relationship — finally a way to compete with Swiggy on owned distribution.
  • The embed flywheel stays intact. Partners get exclusive locked kitchens as their employee/member benefit — that's the offer that makes "MOO at X" valuable to them.
  • Walk-in becomes a real channel, not an afterthought.
  • The nutrition layer becomes the retention loop. Once a customer logs ten meals, the weekly trend is a habit and the suggestions get personalised.

Phased rollout

Phase 1 — Lobby + multi-membership (web/PWA, 1 week)

  • Lobby state when addresses.length === 0.
  • About MOO content shell.
  • Instagram embed.
  • Multi-membership in /profile + active-kitchen switcher in tab shell.
  • Surface invite codes per partner; QR generator on the partner side.

Ships value to customers who already have the app — they can browse the brand even before joining a kitchen.

Phase 2 — Find + Events (1 week)

  • partners.lat/lng + accepts_walkins schema.
  • Geo-find sheet with map.
  • Events table + surface in lobby and member home.

Phase 1 + 2 ship as web. The whole thing remains "Add to Home Screen" installable.

Phase 3 — Nutrition (1 week)

  • Aggregate order_items × recipe-CMS macros into customer_nutrition_daily.
  • Eats tab with daily/weekly view + streak.
  • "What's missing today?" filtered menu suggestions.

Big retention play — the first feature that gives a returning customer a reason to open the app on a non-ordering day.

Phase 4 — Native shell (2 weeks)

  • Capacitor wrap around the existing Next.js app.
  • Push notifications for "your order is being made", "new event at your kitchen", "weekly nutrition recap".
  • Deep links from Slack, IG, partner QR.
  • Apple + Google review process.

This is the milestone that turns "moo.kitchen on someone's home screen" into "a real app you find on the App Store." Worth doing only after phases 1–3 have moved retention metrics in the right direction.

Risks + open questions

  • Native vs PWA. Apple has gotten more permissive about PWAs recently, but no push notifications and a ceiling on perceived legitimacy. Capacitor is the safe pick if app-store presence matters more than time-to-market. Worth deciding before phase 4 starts.
  • Geo + privacy. Asking for location on a food app is fine; storing it is the wrong move. Resolve to an approximate area (PIN code + 3 km radius) and forget the precise coordinate.
  • Nutrition trust. The numbers in Outpost recipe CMS are CSV-driven by the Platform Admin. If the customer-facing nutrition tab is wrong, trust collapses fast. Need a pre-launch QA pass on every active SKU's macros.
  • Walk-in mode for the kitchens. The cloud kitchens were designed for delivery. Turning them into walk-in destinations needs a small ops shift (front-of-house signage, pickup window, walk-in mode in the Outpost tablet UI). Doable, but a real change.
  • Aggregator cannibalisation. If the app shifts even 5 % of aggregator volume to direct, that's a margin win — but it's a real conversation with Swiggy / Zomato about why we're sending traffic away. Worth being intentional about how the deep-links to aggregators are framed in the lobby.

What this is, what this isn't

This is a future-vision sketch, not a roadmap and not a sprint plan. It's the answer to "what could the MOO app feel like a year or two from now if we decided to invert its identity?" — written on May 1 2026, with the v1 backend already running. The phased estimate exists to calibrate scope, not to commit to a date. None of this is funded, on the FY27 plan, or scheduled.

It is not a redesign of v1. The flows that work today (sign-in, slot, menu, cart, Razorpay, tracker, chat) carry forward unchanged. v2 is about adding the surfaces that make the app feel like a brand, not a checkout, and about turning the existing recipe-CMS data into a customer-facing nutrition layer that doesn't exist anywhere else in the Indian breakfast space.
