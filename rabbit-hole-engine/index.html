01 — THE THESIS

Your buyers travel a finite slice of the internet. Map it, win it, and traffic follows.

The internet is effectively infinite. The part that influences your buyer is not — it's a few hundred URLs. Rabbit Hole's first move is to throw away everything else.

Rule 01

Finite

Hundreds of nodes that matter — not the whole web. The map is small enough to reason about.

Rule 02

Living

Refreshed weekly. New nodes appear as the internet moves. Nodes are never deleted — URLs are permanent.

Rule 03

Win rate is the score

The metric: how many nodes moved from absent → present → dominant.

02 — THE MAP

What the map actually looks like

Every dot is a URL your buyer might touch — a Reddit thread, a Google result, a G2 listing, a ChatGPT answer, a comparison page. Colour = your standing on it. This is the showpiece of the platform.

Read the map in one glance

Lots of red around high-traffic hubs = competitors own your buyers' research. Turning red → green is the entire job.

03 — THE THREE THINGS IT OPERATES ON

Everything is a Property, a Node, or a Topic

Three object types make the whole internet computable. Learn these three and you understand the data model.

Entity 01

Property

A domain. reddit.com, g2.com, a competitor's site, your own site. Many nodes live on one property.

  • Type: 1st party (you) / 2nd party (G2, Capterra) / 3rd party (Reddit, blogs, rivals)
  • Domain Authority — how strong the domain is
  • GEO weight — how often LLMs cite it as a source
Entity 02

Node — the atomic unit

A single URL on a property. One Reddit thread, one blog post, one comparison page (real or planned). This is the thing you win or lose.

  • Carries an activation status (see next)
  • Knows its topic, competitors, content depth, SEO rank, GEO-citability
  • Stores a 1–5 value score, effort, dependencies & the next action
Entity 03

Topic

A buyer conversation. "Funnel.io alternatives", "marketing reporting tools". Topics cluster nodes.

  • Search volume, CPC (commercial intent), intent type
  • A ring (Core / Category / Universe)
  • A win rate — % of its nodes you're Present or Dominant on

The moves that climb the ladder

  • Absent → Present: publish content, comment, submit a listing
  • Present → Dominant: optimise, earn reviews, build links
  • Competitor-owned → Contested: show up where only they are

Win rate is a number you can put in front of a client and watch go up every week. "On the 38 nodes that decide this category, you went from 12% present to 41% in 60 days." That's the proof Buyer Search is working.

05 — THE RINGS

Not all conversations are equal — they sit in rings

Topics are organised by how close they are to the buying decision. This is how the engine knows a "vs" page matters more than a thought-leadership essay.

Core — ready to buy

"Clarisights vs Datorama", "Funnel.io alternatives". Highest value — the buyer has their wallet out.

Category — exploring

"cross-channel marketing analytics", "marketing reporting tools". They know the problem, not the vendor.

Universe — thought leadership

"the future of marketing analytics". Awareness & authority, far from the decision.

06 — THE EDGES

The nodes aren't a list — they're connected. That's the IP.

Five kinds of relationship turn a spreadsheet of URLs into a map of buyer journeys. Anyone can list URLs; the edges are what only Rabbit Hole has.

Edge 01

Hyperlink

A links to B — weighted by quality (an editorial backlink ≫ a sidebar link).

Edge 02

SERP co-occurrence

Two URLs that keep ranking together for the same searches.

Edge 03

Co-citation

Two URLs cited together by a third source.

Edge 04

LLM citation

ChatGPT / Claude cite this URL when answering a buyer's question.

Edge 05

Entity co-occurrence

The same brands / products mentioned together across the web.

So what

Edges let you trace a buyer journey: Google → Reddit → comparison page → you. You see the bottlenecks and dead ends.

08 — THE WEEKLY LOOP

The whole machine, run every week

This is the engine in motion. The map ships like software — a new version every Friday.

01

Snapshot

Freeze the whole map — every node, score, status. Like a git commit. v1 = day one (mostly red & grey).

02

Delta

The git-diff of the internet. Splits change into: what we did, what competitors did, what the internet did on its own.

03

Prioritise

Re-rank every gap into a hit list. "Core ring, competitor-owned, 5-star, no blockers" floats to the top.

04

Work

Each node carries an action. Actions collapse into exactly 5 task types (below). Team executes → re-snapshot.

↻  snapshot → delta → prioritise → work → one week later → snapshot …
