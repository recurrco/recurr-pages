Black & white is the brand.
Colour is the school.

Built on Moodboard 1 — "The New Gen": bold, collage, youth-led, high-contrast, real faces. The Quad parent brand stays black-on-white. Each of the three schools owns one loud primary. Three trios below — react, and we iterate.

Space Grotesk (display) · General Sans (body) · Pick a trio →
The principle

One brand, three loud schools.

Walk into any Quad surface and the colour tells you instantly which school you're in — Technology, Management or Design. The typography, collage layout and real faces say "Quad" across all three. Unity + distinction, without three separate brands.

Brand layer (always)

Black #0A0A0A, white, optional paper #F4F2EC. Bold Space Grotesk headlines, General Sans body, collage layouts, real student photography, kinetic hand-drawn marks.

School layer (the colour)

Each school gets ONE primary. It takes over the school's selector tile, landing hero, course cards, and ad creative. The parent Quad logo stays black/white on top of it.

Recommended

Where I need your read

Three open decisions

01

Colour → school mapping

Blue→Technology, Yellow→Management, Coral→Design feels right semantically. Want to swap any? (e.g. Design taking the violet/magenta.)

02

Management as a full primary

Yellow needs black type and can feel less "premium" for the MBA buyer. Keep it loud, or shift Management to a deeper cobalt/orange?

03

Three schools, confirmed?

The board has a "Technology & Management" icon. This system treats them as 3 distinct schools. Locking that before we go further.
