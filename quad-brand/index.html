The idea

Two layers. One identity.

Brown and gold are the enduring face of Quad — warm, grounded, and serious. On top sits a contemporary layer of colour and pattern that keeps the brand ambitious and alive. The first reassures the parent; the second inspires the student. Together they read as an institution with a legacy, not a startup with a logo.

The enduring layer

Solid & prestigious

Brown on cream, one disciplined typeface, all-caps eyebrows under a thin rule, gold used sparingly for emphasis. Generous white space and honest, documentary photography of real students. This never changes — it is what makes Quad feel permanent.

The contemporary layer

Spectrum & pattern

A refined colour spectrum — one hue per school — and four geometric patterns drawn directly from the Quad mark. Used as accents, dividers, and on social, never as noise. This is what makes Quad feel built for what's next.

PATTERN 01

The coordinate plane

Quad begins where you are and plots where you're going. The X-Y axis — arrows reaching outward in every direction — is a system of measurement and direction. Every student's journey is a line on this plane: a clear starting point, a destination, and the deliberate slope between them. Not vague ambition — a plotted trajectory, with proof at every step.

◹ Direction · Measurement · Trajectory
PATTERN 02

The rails for growth

Parallel lines, running on without end — the infrastructure a student needs to grow. These are the rails: the structure of the programme, the shape of the curriculum, the arc of a career laid out ahead. They don't stop at graduation. They run infinitely forward, carrying momentum from the first day to a lifetime of compounding progress.

▤ Structure · Infrastructure · Infinite growth
PATTERN 03

Concentric reach

Circles radiating outward from a single centre — the signature of growth and reach. What begins as one student, one idea, one campus ripples outward: into community, into industry, into the world. Each ring is wider than the last. Quad is built to expand — and to keep expanding, long after the first ripple.

◎ Growth · Reach · Momentum outward
PATTERN 04

The network of dots

A field of points, connected — community, cohorts, data, and network. Every dot is a student, a mentor, a recruiter, a data point in a living system. Alone, a point. Together, a network with real gravity. This is the social fabric of Quad, and the raw material of a school designed for an AI-native world.

⠿ Community · Cohorts · Network · Data

Photography & social

Real students. Real campus. One treatment.

Quad's strength is honest, documentary photography — actual students mid-work, presenting, building. The raw shots are authentic but inconsistent. The system fixes that with one repeatable treatment: wash the image in the school accent, lay one pattern over it, and lock the same headline frame every time.

Actual imagery from quadcse.com — candid, warm, real faces. This is the raw material; never replace it with stock.

The social treatment

The accent colour does the heavy lifting — a strong wash that unifies any photo into the school's identity — with a pattern motif layered over it in gold and a consistent headline lockup.

Life at Quad

Made at Quad. Built for what's next.

Do

  • Wash images heavily in the school accent — own the colour
  • Layer one pattern motif over the photo, in gold
  • Shoot real students, real moments — candid over posed
  • Crop tight on faces, hands, and the work itself
  • Lock the same eyebrow-bar + headline frame every time

Don't

  • Use generic stock or AI-generated "students"
  • Leave the photo un-washed or use a weak, washed-out tint
  • Stack two patterns or let the motif fight the face
  • Crowd the frame — protect the scrim and breathing room
  • Set headlines in a different font or all-caps blocks
Where I need your read

Open decisions before v1.0

01

Brown + Gold balance

Brown is primary, gold the signature. Comfortable with brown as the dominant face, or should gold carry more weight on hero surfaces?

02

School → hue mapping

Technology = Teal, Management = Crimson, Design = Violet — all tuned to sit with brown/gold. Any swaps, or want a fourth reserved for a future school?

03

Motif → school pairing

Right now all four patterns are shared. Worth assigning each school a signature motif (e.g. Technology = coordinate plane, Management = rails)?

04

Companion palettes

Each school now has a 5-colour set. Are these the right companions, or do you want tighter / wider ranges per school?
