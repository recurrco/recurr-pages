Data Literacy — Layer 2: Industry Playbooks

The "utility" layer of the Data Literacy program. Where L1 defines the language (glossary, metric & platform pages), L2 turns it into industry-specific playbooks a marketing team can actually run on — the layer that converts a reader into a believer. 30 articles = 6 verticals × 5 sections.
Format: PlaybookUtility contentIndustry-led, not platform-ledSME-grounded
00 · What it isThe premise

Buyers self-identify by industry — not by platform.

A performance marketer at a fintech doesn't search "how to use Meta Ads reporting." They search "fintech marketing KPIs" and "how do other fintechs structure their reporting." L2 meets them there: practical, vertical-specific playbooks that teach the craft of marketing data — useful even if the reader never touches Clarisights.

Each playbook is grounded in real customer operations (CS conversations + Zulip threads), not marketing brainstorms. The product sells itself when the reader realises the standard they just learned is already built into the platform.

L2 is the proof-of-competence layer. L1 shows we know the words; L2 shows we know how the best teams in your industry actually run the data.
01 · The Model5 sections per vertical

Every vertical gets the same five sections.

One repeatable template, filled with industry-specific substance. Ship it once (eCommerce), then replicate the pattern five times.
1
KPI Framework
What to measure & why. Hierarchy from board → CMO → channel manager. Benchmarks + what "good" looks like at scale.
2
Campaign Naming Standard
Naming syntax with real industry examples. UTM taxonomy that scales multi-market. The Data Standards reference.
3
Reporting Templates
Three real downloads: weekly (channel-manager) · monthly (CMO) · quarterly (board). Grounded in real customer reports.
4
Data Setup Guide
What to connect first, which dimensions from day zero, which transformations — the 3 setup decisions that shape everything downstream.
5
Common Data Mistakes
The 5–7 failure modes specific to the industry, pulled from Zulip + CS threads. Each mistake gets a fix.

04 · PrinciplesThe moat

Why this compounds — the graph, not the page.

  • Specificity over scale. No "Top 10 KPIs" listicles. Every page answers a specific question a specific marketer has at a specific moment.
  • Nomenclature is the thesis, not a footnote. Naming, field taxonomy and API conventions are the central argument — that's what makes it defensible.
  • Teach the craft, not the tool. Every piece is useful even if the reader never uses Clarisights. The product sells itself once they see the standard is built in.
  • Grounded in real operations. CS conversations and Zulip threads are the source material — the SME column on every row is the authority.
  • The moat is the interconnected graph. Every L2 playbook references the L1 definitions; every L3 authority piece drives back to both. Individual pages capture traffic; the graph builds authority nobody can shortcut.
05 · Reality checkDependencies & status

What has to be true for L2 to ship.

  • Status: all 30 planned, none drafted. The proof-point (eCommerce, 5 sections incl. 3 template downloads) is the first thing to write.
  • Publishing home. L2 cannot go live until press.clarisights.com exists (July). Drafting is not blocked; publishing is.
  • L1 first. The graph logic assumes the L1 glossary/metric pages exist to link into. L1 leads, L2 references it.
  • SME time. Each playbook needs its CS anchor (Avi/Sufyaan) and Philip for the naming standard. SME availability is the real production constraint, not writing capacity.
  • A named DRI. The content engine still runs on an unnamed [account lead] — assign an owner before scaling past the eCommerce proof point.
Fastest path to value: draft the eCommerce 5 now so they're ready the day the publishing home is live — and use that vertical as the template every other vertical clones.
