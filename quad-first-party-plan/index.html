01 · The lensOne frame, three layers

The internet has three layers. You should think of them differently.

A useful frame before we talk about the work. Every place a buyer can encounter Quad sits in one of these three layers — and each one demands a different kind of effort, ownership, and patience.

Layer 01

First-partyThe internet you own.

Your domain, your pages, your published content, your CMS, your database. You decide what goes up, when it goes up, and how it changes.

quad.ac.in · landing pages · long-form · programmatic SEO · alumni stories
Layer 02

Second-partyThe internet you lease.

Surfaces where you have a profile and can publish directly — but the platform owns the audience and the rules. You rent the reach.

LinkedIn · YouTube · Instagram · Reddit (your account) · X · email lists
Layer 03

Third-partyThe internet you don't post on.

Surfaces you cannot publish to directly. Someone else has to publish on your behalf — a journalist, a creator, a redditor, an alum. You influence; they execute.

Press · media coverage · creator videos · Quora answers · Reddit threads (others') · Shiksha / CollegeDunia
Why this matters: Most institutes spend on layer 2 and hope layer 3 happens. Almost none take layer 1 seriously. Layer 1 is the only one you fully control — and it's the only one that compounds.
02 · The diagnosisWhere Quad is weakest
Today's weakest arm

First-party is the muscle to build first.

It's where the gap is largest, where you have full control, and where every other layer eventually points back to. First-party has two parts. Quad is weak on both.

Part 01 · Infra

The plumbing to publish at speed.

A real CMS your team can actually use. Modular content models. Studio-style builds inside it so writing, editing, reviewing, and publishing don't bottleneck on engineering.

The proposal: Stand up Sanity (or equivalent) with multiple studio surfaces — one per content programme — so the team can keep processing volume without blocking each other.
Part 02 · Content production

Programmes, not posts.

Not one-off articles. Standing programmes — each one with a defined audience, a defined cadence, and a defined outcome. Most institutes have zero. The category winners have three to five.

The proposal: Four to five dedicated content programmes published directly on the Quad site. Detailed on the next slide.
The order matters: Infra first, because every programme on the next slide assumes a publishing surface that today doesn't exist. Two to three weeks of plumbing buys twelve months of velocity.

05 · SequencingMonths 2 — 12 · Bottoms-up, always

We rank from the bottom up. Never the other way around.

Top-of-funnel traffic without a converting funnel underneath is a leaky bucket. We start by ranking on the lowest-volume / highest-intent queries — proving the conversion path works — before we spend a single rupee of effort scaling reach. Once the bottom converts, we expand upward.

Phase 01
Bottom of funnelMonths 2 — 3

Rank where the buyer is already deciding.

Lowest volume. Highest intent. If these don't convert, no amount of upstream traffic will save the model.

What we publish
  • "Quad vs Scaler" / "Quad vs Newton" / "Quad vs NxtWave"
  • "Is Quad legit / accredited / worth it"
  • Parent-anxiety pages (fees, ROI, placements)
  • Alumni outcomes & placement deep-dives
  • Application FAQs & admissions clarity pages
Why first: these are the people closest to writing the cheque. Cheapest to win, fastest to prove the bucket isn't leaky.
Phase 02
Mid-funnelMonths 4 — 6

Win the evaluation set.

Mid volume. Decision-stage searches. We scale only after BoFu is converting — confidence earned, not assumed.

What we publish
  • "Best CSE / tech-ed colleges in India 2026"
  • "Alternatives if I didn't get IIT / NIT"
  • "How to evaluate a new-age tech institute"
  • Programmatic SEO goes live (200 — 500 pages)
  • First press-driven third-party coverage lands
Why second: by now we've proven Quad's pages convert. We earn the right to spend on volume.
Phase 03
Top of funnelMonths 7 — 12

Earn the reach.

Highest volume. Lowest intent. The expensive layer. We only spend here once the bottom and middle are demonstrably working.

What we publish
  • Founder-led essays · category-defining
  • Parent-therapy series at scale
  • pSEO scales to 1,000+ indexed pages
  • Tier-1 media & creator coverage (third-party)
  • Ecosystem & school-network content (8 — 12 cohort)
Why last: top-of-funnel traffic compounds — but only when there is a converting bottom for it to land on.
The discipline: at any review, the question is not "did traffic grow" — it's "did the lowest-funnel layer rank higher and convert better than last month." Volume is a lagging output, not a leading one.
06 · Who owns whatRecurr's role across the three layers

Recurr's depth of ownership changes per layer.

We are not pretending to execute everything. On first-party, we own end-to-end. On second-party, we ideate and you publish from your handles. On third-party, we ideate and the world publishes — we shape what gets distributed; we cannot guarantee distribution itself.

Layer Recurr's role What this looks like
First-partyOwned 100% execution All five content programmes, on quad.ac.in. We build the infra, run the studios, write, edit, publish, measure. Thinking and execution both sit with us.
Second-partyLeased We drive LinkedIn, Reddit (Quad's account), YouTube, X. We ideate, brief, draft. Your team publishes from your profiles. We can drive cadence; we don't own the channel.
Third-partyEarned We guide Press & media coverage, creator videos, third-party Reddit / Quora threads. We brief, pitch, and shape what's worth publishing. The publishing itself happens through journalists, creators, and the broader internet.
Read this honestly: First-party is where we're accountable end-to-end. Second-party is a partnership. Third-party is influence — high upside, lower certainty. The plan front-loads the layer where we have the most control.
07 · Bandwidth askWhat we need from your side

Recurr is end-to-end on first-party. Two things have to come from your side.

Small in calendar terms, decisive in outcome terms. If these two are present, the engine runs at the cadence on slide 03. If they're missing, the work stalls regardless of how much we ship.

Ask 01

Subject-matter context.

When we write about a programme, an alumnus, a placement number, a curriculum decision, or anything happening inside Quad — we need a fast way to validate facts with someone on your side. Not for everything; only where we can't get to the truth from public information.

One designated person, async by default, weekly review by exception.

~2 hours per week · one named owner
Ask 02

Last-mile distribution.

We do not own distribution. We create first-party content; second-party distribution (LinkedIn, Reddit, YouTube, X) needs to publish from your handles. If you have a distribution team — even one person — we'll brief them precisely on what to post, where, and when. They run the last mile.

Without this, second-party reach is capped at what your founders post personally.

1 distribution owner · ideally a small team
Bonus · if you want it

We will fill your distribution team's hands.

Beyond just distributing first-party content, we can build a complete second-party content stream tailored to your team's capacity — daily LinkedIn posts, weekly YouTube shorts, Reddit-ready answers, X threads, comment-thread playbooks. One brief, structured library, ready to ship.

If your distribution team has extra hands, we will fill them with work. The output of one team becomes the firehose for the next, and second-party reach scales with team size — not just with our hours.

Optional · activated on request · scales with your headcount
08 · The outcomeWhat good looks like at month twelve

Two metrics. Twelve months. No vanity.

We deliberately leave output volume out of this. Output is a three-week early indicator at best — useful for our team, not for yours. These two are the metrics that actually represent inbound visibility being earned.

Metric 01 · Traffic
100K/ month
Monthly organic visits to quad.ac.in by the end of month 12. Almost entirely driven by the first-party programmes — long-form, parent content, programmatic SEO. The number compounds; the early months will look small.
Measured · Google Analytics + Search Console · monthly
Metric 02 · AI citation share
Top 5· on 10–20 keywords
A defined set of 10–20 keywords Quad chooses to fully own. Target: Quad shows up in the top five cited sources when ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, or Claude answer those queries. The exact keyword set is a week-one exercise.
Measured · LLM citation tracking · weekly
M3 checkpoint
Top-5 on BoFu cluster.
"Quad vs Scaler / Newton / NxtWave" + 5 — 10 brand-direct queries ranking top-5 on Google. AI citation begins appearing.
M6 checkpoint
Mid-funnel base built.
200 — 500 pSEO pages indexed. 25 — 40K monthly organic. AI citation share crosses 30% on the chosen keyword set.
M12 north star
Inbound at 100K / month.
1,000+ indexed pages. Top-5 AI citation on the chosen 10 — 20 keywords. Inbound leads visibly compounding off organic.
Why these two: Traffic is the cumulative test of every layer working together. AI citation is the leading test of whether the content is good enough to be quoted by the systems your buyers will increasingly start their search on. Both are visible to you, both are unfakeable, neither rewards busywork.
