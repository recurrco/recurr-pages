01 — Executive Summary

The Big Finding

Nobody owns "The Standard" for paid marketing data. Despite billions spent on marketing analytics tools, no competitor has built a comprehensive, open, platform-neutral data literacy resource. The territory is wide open.

Key patterns across all 8 competitors:

  • Glossaries exist but are shallow. Most are SEO plays with 1-2 paragraph definitions. None provide cross-platform metric comparisons (e.g., "here's how Facebook, Google, and TikTok each define a 'conversion').
  • Naming conventions are covered by only 3 of 8 competitors (Improvado, Adverity, Supermetrics). Even those are blog posts, not interactive tools or enforced standards.
  • Data normalization/harmonization is Improvado's strongest territory. They have 5+ deep articles on this exact topic. Everyone else is surface-level.
  • Templates are Supermetrics' moat. They have the largest template gallery (90+ templates across Looker Studio, Sheets, Excel, Power BI, BigQuery). Funnel.io is second with ~7 Looker Studio templates.
  • Industry-specific content is Improvado's play. Dedicated industry pages for Retail, Fintech, etc. with vertical-specific KPI guides. Nobody else goes vertical.
  • No one has a "metric comparison engine" showing how the same metric is defined across different ad platforms. This is the single biggest gap.
  • Mobile attribution players (AppsFlyer, Singular) have the best glossaries but only cover mobile. Paid media broadly has no equivalent.

05 — Content Gap Analysis

What NOBODY is doing well, organized by opportunity size.

Gap 1: Cross-Platform Metric Definitions

NOBODY HAS THIS

No competitor has a resource that shows: "Here's how Facebook defines a 'conversion' vs how Google defines it vs how TikTok defines it." Every existing glossary defines terms in isolation. Paid marketers working across 5-10 platforms desperately need this. This is the single most differentiated thing Clarisights can build.

What to build: A metric comparison engine. For each metric (CPC, CPM, CPA, ROAS, conversion, impression, etc.), show the exact definition from each major platform, highlight differences, and explain normalization logic.

Gap 2: Platform-Neutral Data Standard

NOBODY HAS THIS

No one has positioned their content as "the open standard" for marketing data. IAB has technical measurement standards (OM SDK, ADMaP, ECAPI) but these are for ad tech infrastructure, not for marketers. The space between "IAB technical specs" and "vendor blog posts" is completely empty. This is where "The Open Standard for Paid Marketing Data" lives.

What to build: A formal, versioned, open-access standard document that defines how paid marketing data should be structured, named, and normalized. Think of it as OpenAPI spec, but for marketing data.

Gap 3: Interactive Naming Convention Builder

BLOG POSTS EXIST, NO TOOL

Improvado, Adverity, and Supermetrics have blog posts about naming conventions. Adverity has a product feature. But nobody has a free, open, interactive tool that helps you BUILD your naming convention. CampaignTrackly is the closest (it's their whole product), but it's a paid SaaS tool, not a free resource.

What to build: An open naming convention builder: pick your platforms, choose your taxonomy elements (geo, channel, campaign type, audience, creative variant), get a generated convention with examples. Downloadable as a Google Sheet or CSV template.

Gap 4: Industry/Vertical Playbooks for Data

IMPROVADO HAS PARTIAL

Improvado has industry pages (Retail, Fintech) and vertical KPI guides. But these are about marketing strategy, not about data. Nobody has: "Here's the data model for an eCommerce paid media operation" or "Here's how a SaaS company should structure their marketing data." The gap is data architecture by vertical, not marketing tips by vertical.

What to build: Industry playbooks focused on data: which metrics matter per vertical, how to structure naming conventions per industry, which platform integrations are critical, sample dashboards by vertical.

Gap 5: "What Changed" Platform Update Tracker

NOBODY HAS THIS

Ad platforms constantly change their APIs, metrics, and attribution models. Nobody maintains a running changelog of platform data changes that affect marketers. Google deprecating a metric, Meta changing attribution windows, TikTok adding new dimensions — marketers find out when dashboards break.

What to build: A living changelog: "Platform Data Changes That Affect Your Reporting." Updated weekly/monthly. Would become a must-bookmark for every data-driven marketer.

Gap 6: Comprehensive Paid Media Glossary (non-mobile)

NOBODY HAS THIS FOR PAID MEDIA

AppsFlyer has 100+ mobile terms. Singular has 30-50 mobile terms. For non-mobile paid media (Google Ads, Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok, programmatic, retail media), the best glossaries come from small agencies (Inovaticus with 300+ terms, Loganix with 200+ PPC terms). No major analytics platform has built a comprehensive, authoritative, well-designed paid media glossary.

06 — Recommendations — What to Build First

Prioritized by competitive gap size, SEO opportunity, and alignment with Clarisights positioning.

P0 — BUILD FIRST (Month 1)

The Paid Marketing Data Glossary

200+ terms. Cross-platform definitions showing how each ad platform defines the same metric differently. A-Z navigation, search, individual term pages for SEO. Include: formulas, examples, platform-specific notes, and "common mistakes." This alone would rank for "marketing analytics glossary," "paid media glossary," and dozens of long-tail terms that currently have zero strong competition.

Why first: No competitor ranks for glossary terms. AppsFlyer's mobile glossary proves the model works. The cross-platform comparison angle is completely unique and directly tied to Clarisights' product value.

Reference models: AppsFlyer glossary (best structure), Triple Whale glossary (good A-Z UX), Singular glossary (video integration).

P0 — BUILD FIRST (Month 1)

Campaign Naming Convention Guide + Interactive Builder

A comprehensive guide that goes beyond "best practices" (what Improvado and Adverity already have) to an interactive tool. Let marketers select their platforms, pick taxonomy elements, and generate a naming convention. Include downloadable templates. This would immediately outclass the existing blog posts from competitors.

Why first: 3 competitors already rank for this. CampaignTrackly is the only "tool" and it's paid. A free, open tool from Clarisights would generate massive leads and establish the "standard" positioning immediately.

P1 — BUILD SECOND (Month 2)

Platform Data Guides (one per ad platform)

For each major platform (Google Ads, Meta/Facebook, TikTok, LinkedIn, Snapchat, Pinterest, programmatic/DV360, Amazon Ads, Apple Search Ads), create a comprehensive guide covering: available metrics and their exact definitions, API data model, common data quality issues, normalization recommendations, naming convention templates. These become the canonical reference for each platform's data.

Why second: Nobody has platform-specific data guides. Closest is Supermetrics' platform-specific templates, but those are reports, not data documentation. This is the "standard" made practical.

P1 — BUILD SECOND (Month 2)

Data Normalization Playbook

A practical guide to normalizing marketing data across platforms. Cover: currency normalization, timezone alignment, metric harmonization (clicks, conversions, impressions), campaign structure mapping. Include decision trees and code examples. Directly compete with Improvado's 5+ articles but consolidate into one definitive resource.

Why second: Improvado owns this SERP. But their content is spread across 5+ articles. A single, definitive, better-designed resource could take the #1 position, especially if it includes interactive elements and downloadable templates.

P2 — BUILD THIRD (Month 3)

Industry Playbooks (eCommerce first, then SaaS)

Data-focused industry guides. Start with eCommerce (compete with Triple Whale's content but focus on data infrastructure, not metrics). Then SaaS. Cover: recommended data model, key metrics by vertical, naming convention templates, sample dashboards, platform priorities, and benchmarks.

P2 — BUILD THIRD (Month 3+)

Platform Change Tracker

Living document tracking ad platform data changes. Updated weekly. Low effort to maintain, high bookmark value. Could become a newsletter. No competitor does this — it's pure blue ocean content.
