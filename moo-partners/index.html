Ministry of Oats — The Partner Pipeline Internal · Operating model · May 20, 2026

Five lanes. One partner pipeline.

Every MOO partner has a stage. They move up the lanes over time. The deepest lane bridges into the chain's own store expansion — meaning the partner pipeline is not just B2B2C distribution, it's a discovery path for new walk-in locations.

Why a pipeline, not a partnerships list

Until now, partner work has been ad-hoc — "MOO at X" pages, Fridges, events, kiosks, all living in the same overflowing folder. The pipeline gives every partner a stage, a clear next move, and a measurable velocity. Same way a B2B sales team runs a CRM. Partners are a deal pipeline.

Lane by lane

What each lane looks like in practice.

Lane 1

In touch

Discovery. We're talking, nothing has happened yet.

What it looks like

  • Cold or warm intro call with a partner contact
  • Brand-side interest expressed — "we'd love to do something with MOO"
  • Initial brief shared — what they might want, what fits
  • No commercials, no transactions

Where MOO shows up

A deck, an intro page, a sample menu. Sometimes a coffee meeting.

Advance signal — L1 → L2 First real transaction agreed: a sampling session, a single event, a one-off bulk order.
Lane 2

Activated

One transaction done. We have proof we can work together.

What it looks like

  • Took a kiosk to their venue for an event
  • Processed a bulk order and delivered to their location
  • Ran a sampling Sunday at their gym / club / society
  • Catered a partnership event

Where MOO shows up

Physically present, briefly. Their members or staff have tasted MOO. Photos, social tags, follow-up nudges.

Advance signal — L2 → L3 Three or more transactions in six months, OR an explicit request for a recurring custom-menu relationship.
Lane 3

Custom menu (MOO at X)

A partner-specific menu, a branded link, an ongoing relationship.

What it looks like

  • Custom menu built for the partner — usually a curated subset of MOO with partner-relevant additions (post-workout for a gym, family-friendly for a society, etc.)
  • Branded link / page on ministryofoats.com (e.g., /partner-slug)
  • Recurring ordering — weekly, monthly, or on-demand
  • Internal account opened on Outpost ops for fulfilment tracking

Where MOO shows up

A regular feature in their orbit. Their members order from MOO often enough that we're part of the routine.

Advance signal — L3 → L4 Stable monthly recurring volume, partner-side appetite for an on-site presence, physical space available for a Fridge.
Lane 4

Fridge

A MOO Fridge inside their space. Stocked daily. Members order via wallet.

What it looks like

  • Physical Fridge placed at the partner location — gym lobby, club, society common area, corporate pantry
  • Stocked daily by MOO ops (delivery 7-11 AM, fits the breakfast window)
  • Members order via QR / MOO wallet at the Fridge or via the membership site
  • Partner gets co-branded visibility + share-of-foot-traffic value
  • MOO gets a daily-touch surface in someone else's high-traffic space

Where MOO shows up

A permanent fixture. Every morning, MOO is on display in their space. The Fridge is the brand's face inside the partnership.

Advance signal — L4 → L5 High Fridge throughput at a single partner site, partner-side appetite for a deeper co-located brand presence, foot traffic that justifies a dedicated retail format.
Lane 5

Retail at venue

A MOO walk-in store, inside the partner's footprint.

What it looks like

  • A dedicated MOO retail outlet at the partner's venue — could be a kiosk (Urban Vault format, ~350 sft) or a small QSR-format store
  • Co-branded, but MOO-run (staff, menu, brand)
  • Open to the partner's members + the general public
  • Partner provides space (often at favourable terms — anchor tenant economics); MOO provides the brand + footfall
  • Operates as one of the chain's regular walk-in locations

Where MOO shows up

This is the chain meeting the partnership. A MOO store with a partner address. The partner gets a premium amenity; MOO gets a walk-in location with the lease + anchor-tenant economics already de-risked.

L5 = the chain expansion bridge Lane 5 partners can become walk-in stores for the chain without MOO landing its own lease. The Urban Vault partnership currently sits between L4 (Fridges in UV buildings) and L5 (a MOO kiosk at a UV space). When we open kiosks 1 and 2 at UV, we're shipping the first L5s. The math: every L5 we ship is one less new lease we have to land for the chain's 3 → 8 store expansion.

How the pipeline moves

Progression + operating model.

What it takes to advance a lane

  • L1 → L2 First transaction agreed — a sampling, a single event, a one-off bulk order.
  • L2 → L3 3+ transactions in 6 months OR an explicit custom-menu ask.
  • L3 → L4 Stable monthly recurring volume + space for a Fridge + partner buy-in.
  • L4 → L5 High single-site Fridge throughput + partner appetite for retail + a footprint that supports a walk-in format.

Lane velocity — how fast partners move up — becomes a key metric. A partner stuck at L1 for 6 months is dead weight; a partner at L4 in 4 months is a story we need to tell.

How the pipeline runs operationally

  • Tooling CRM-style view of every partner record, current lane, history, owner. Lives inside the Outpost ops platform OR a dedicated tool (Attio is the existing stack and an obvious candidate).
  • Ownership L1–L3 likely lives with the brand/partnerships team (Rushil). L4–L5 escalates to ops + leadership (Pratik, Manjunath). Lane assignment + lane transitions are explicit hand-offs.
  • Cadence Weekly pipeline standup. Monthly velocity review. Quarterly pipeline-shape review (are we top-heavy at L1, or moving partners through?).
  • Capacity Each lane has limited slots — Fridges (L4) cost capital; retail (L5) needs ops bandwidth. Pipeline prioritisation matters more than pipeline volume.
What this replaces

The "MOO at X" mess collapses into a clean operating model.

Until today, partner work has lived under loose labels — "MOO at X" / "Partner Gangs" / "the Fridge motion" / "events business" / "the Urban Vault thing." The 5-lane pipeline puts them all on one map.

  • "MOO at X" is L3 — the custom-menu stage.
  • "Partner Gangs / Fridge motion" is L4 — the on-site Fridge stage.
  • The Urban Vault partnership currently sits between L4 and L5 — Fridges in UV buildings (L4), with the first kiosks at UV spaces shipping as our first L5s.
  • "Events business" is L2 — single-transaction activations that may or may not turn into recurring relationships.
  • Stalled deals everyone forgot about — that's L1, where partners go to die unless we work them.
