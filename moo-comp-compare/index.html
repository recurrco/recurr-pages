MOO vs Comps — Forward Revenue Multiple

Early-stage consumer-QSR rounds price on forward revenue, not trailing. Because these brands grow 3–6× a year, a round that looks like 8–15× trailing is really ~2–6× the next-12-month number. Here's what the real precedents priced at — and where MOO's ask sits.

Multiple = post-money ÷ next-12-month (forward) gross revenue · apples-to-apples: all revenues are gross customer sales (aggregator commission booked as cost)
Median ≈ 3.0× forward · MOO at ₹24–30 Cr = 2.3–2.9× The comps clear a median ~3.0× / average ~3.5× forward revenue. MOO's ask is at or below the median — while being profitable, which almost none of them are. Pricing MOO at the comp average (~3.3×) implies ₹34–36 Cr post.

Why MOO deserves the low multiple

  • Profitable today — company EBITDA +3% (clean months); the comps in this band are burning.
  • 60% repeat — abnormal for QSR; the traction proof point.
  • Priced at 2.3–2.9× forward vs a 3.0× median — a clean, evidence-backed discount.
  • Comp-average (~3.3×) would justify ₹34–36 Cr post — headroom above the ask.

What an investor will press

  • The ₹10.4 Cr forward rests on the rollout (new kitchens + fridge/QSR + Delhi).
  • Haircut to the proven cloud-kitchen path (~₹6–7 Cr) → MOO rises to ~3.5–4.5×, into the comp average — still defensible.
  • Forward numbers are believed, not banked — same for every comp (Boba's ₹70 Cr was "eyeing ₹100 Cr").
  • Growth is unit-multiplication, not same-store — flat volume, rising basket.
