Section 01 · The Buyer01 / 27

Who's actually buying, and from which budget.

There is no buyer with a "GTM Execution Platform" budget line. There is one budget, five seats around it, and a CRO who buys exactly one thing. We start there.
1.1 · The Buying Committee02 / 27

One budget. Five seats around it.

In a B2B SaaS company between 100 and 1,000 FTE — Kris's natural ICP — five roles touch the decision. A champion drives it, a CRO funds it, RevOps blocks it, end users vote with adoption, and PMM whispers in the CRO's ear.
Champion
Head of Sales Development
SDR Manager · Director of Outbound. The person whose number gets hit or missed by Kris.
Picks tool · escalates
Economic Buyer
VP Sales · CRO
Cares about pipeline coverage, cost-per-meeting, stack consolidation. Signs the cheque.
Signs · veto-on-budget
Technical Buyer
RevOps Director
Owns Salesforce/HubSpot integration depth, data quality, governable AI. Will block on integration risk.
Veto-on-stack
End User
SDR · AE · CSM
Wants less manual research, less Salesforce data entry, faster sequence build. Refusal-to-adopt kills renewals.
Veto-on-adoption
Influencer
PMM · Demand Gen
Owns intent-signal coordination, ABM tie-ins, attribution back to pipeline. Whispers to the CRO.
Recommends · advocates
The CRO buys one thing: more pipeline, at a lower cost-per-meeting, on a stack that is shrinking, not growing. Everything else is a feature claim that has to ladder up to that.
Frame this slide for the PMM: she is selling to a CRO who has been told by every vendor since 2024 that AI will fix outbound. Trust is at an all-time low. The Companion framing matters. The proof matters more.

2.4 · Build vs Buy vs Platform10 / 27

The actual debate happening in the CRO's room.

The PMM at Kris will recognise this immediately. Three paths exist; only one is alive in 2026; and the one alive is already crowded.
Path A · Build in-house

Custom GenAI workflows on top of OpenAI / Anthropic APIs

Tried hard in 2024–2025. Mostly abandoned by Q4 2025 — too expensive to maintain, no signal data, no deliverability infra, no governance.

Status: Dead path. Internal teams now want vendors.

Path B · Old point-tool stack

Apollo + Outreach + ZoomInfo + Gong + 6sense

$300K–$700K/yr for a 50-rep org, with 2-3 RevOps engineers maintaining integrations. CFOs killing this. Stack consolidation became a 2026 board-level metric.

Status: Under attack. Renewals slipping.

Path C · New unified platform

Agentic GTM platform — Apollo, Outreach, Kris

This is the door Kris is walking through. Apollo got there 6 months earlier with much louder marketing. Outreach is sprinting. Kris has 60-90 days to occupy the Companion frame before Apollo claims it too.

Status: Open · narrowing.

The Recurr Press piece writes itself: "After 11x: the Companion vs Autonomous SDR debate." Drives PR + LinkedIn for Arun and Ramakrishna. We'll outline this in section 4.

4.4 · What this plan is not22 / 27

What this plan is not.

Equally important. Boundary-setting protects the 90-day commitment.
  1. Not category creation. "AI Work Companion" is fine as branding, but no inbound budget chases <10 mo searches. We anchor to the buyer's lexicon — Outreach alternative, AI SDR, agentic GTM — and let Kris's category sit on top.
  2. Not 26 essays. Pillar + supporting essays is one workstream of four, not the whole plan. A blog stuffed with category content with no comparison or listicle layer underneath produces zero pipeline.
  3. Not paid acquisition. Bottom-of-funnel CPCs are $146–$152. Inbound has to be organic + AEO + listicle. Paid is a Q3 conversation once organic has a baseline.
  4. Not Sujith's old keyword list. The March 2026 universe was built before Apollo locked agentic GTM and before 11x collapsed. The map is redrawn around the post-March reality.
  5. Not a 12-month commitment. 90 days, three numbers, re-cut at Day 90. PMM-defensible to a CRO.
If any of the three north-star outcomes is off-target at Day 90, the engagement is re-scoped or ended. This is the deal.
