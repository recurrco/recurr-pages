A · Audit
What's actually broken

The business is generating leads. The business is not converting leads. The fault is not in the market — it's in the plumbing.

A.1 · Lead flow

The lead flow is broken. Three biggest problems.

1

No single inbound layer

A lead on the dental site goes to Freshworks and never lands in Zenoti. A salon lead goes to Zenoti. A clinic lead goes to Instapracto. A WhatsApp lead goes to Freshworks. Four disconnected ponds, zero flow between them. You cannot automate four CRMs. You can automate one.

2

Response time kills the pipeline

Leads wait up to three hours for a human reply. Off-hours leads (12 AM – 1:30 AM — a real slot for this customer) get nothing: no bot, no fallback, no triage. In beauty and aesthetics, the first brand to reply wins. You are losing before the conversation starts.

3

No definition of "resolved"

~10,000 conversations are marked resolved in Freshworks with no conversion tracking attached. A closed ticket is not a closed customer. The team has no idea which channel, which campaign, which message actually produced revenue — so every decision is a guess.

Consequence

The team can't see the funnel, so they can't fix the funnel. They keep adding spend at the top instead of plugging the holes in the middle.

A.2 · Web properties

Multiple web properties are working against you

Three websites (ZBL, ZAC, ZDL), three domains, three design languages. The clinic's booking is on a bolt.host prototype URL. The dental site's WhatsApp button is dead. The salon's Zenoti webstore failed to load on a live test.

  • 01SEO is split three ways. Three weak domains instead of one strong one. Domain authority — the thing that compounds — never gets to compound.
  • 02The customer sees three brands instead of one. A woman who walks into Villa 7 for a facial has no idea there's an aesthetic clinic 30 steps away.
  • 03Paid spend is three times more expensive than it needs to be. Every channel pushes traffic to three destinations that should be one.
  • 04Ops is three times more expensive than it needs to be. Three sites, three content calendars, three SEO programs, three analytics setups.

One property. One brand. One destination.

A.4 · UGC

UGC and forums — other brands are getting hyped, Zieda is not

Search Reddit (r/dubai, r/beauty, r/30PlusSkinCare), Quora, beauty forums, expat groups. Look for "best aesthetic clinic Dubai", "where to get botox Dubai", "Zieda reviews". The signal is near-silent. Meanwhile, competitors — including ones with weaker actual service — have organic threads, recommendations, community chatter.

Why UGC is the conviction layer for luxury

A customer spending 4,000–5,000 AED on a single visit does not trust an ad. She trusts what a stranger wrote about her own experience. UGC compounds — an ad stops the moment you stop paying; a Reddit thread from 2026 still converts in 2028.

The line we want strangers to write

"If you have the money for it, you should go to Zieda." Not "Zieda is the best" (too generic, sounds paid). The phrasing signals luxury — scarcity and aspiration in one sentence.

A.5 · Personal brand

The personal brand strategy makes sense — and a competitor is proving it

Three businesses were born out of one thing: Zieda Sharipova's personal reputation. Her closed personal account — roughly 100 followers — drives single bills of 4,000–5,000 AED. That is a signal the market is screaming: we want to buy because of her, not in spite of her. Then the group split into three brands and everyone forgot the origin.

The inverted Kardashian — credibility first, glamour second. Hairstylist → Creative Director → Founder → Expert. Science + storytelling. "Started from the bottom" is earned, not manufactured.

Who is already winning this playbook

Huda Kattan · Huda Beauty

$1B+ empire built on personal brand → blog → Instagram → product line → retail. Same region, same category. Exact template.

Dr. Sandra Lee · Dr. Pimple Popper

Aesthetic dermatologist → personal content → media empire → product line → TV show. Direct template for a clinic founder.

Lucia Clinic

Softer version of the playbook — founder-forward content doing heavy lifting. Even the soft version outperforms faceless clinic marketing.

The personal brand is not a vanity project. It is the cheapest and most defensible growth channel Zieda has — and it's sitting unused.

B · Recommendations
What "good" looks like

Zieda at the centre. One solid unified platform. A magazine that runs on Instagram and a website.

B.1 · The model

Zieda at the centre. One platform. A magazine, not a brochure.

Stop thinking of Zieda as three businesses with three websites. Start thinking of Zieda as a beauty magazine with a shopfront. The magazine runs on two surfaces — Instagram and a single website — and Zieda herself is the editor-in-chief, the face, and the main story.

  • 01One unified domain. One design language. One editorial voice. One CMS.
  • 02The website is not a brochure, it is a magazine — stories, education, before/afters, founder point of view, Russian skincare rituals, peer experiences, treatment explainers. Booking lives on the same surface.
  • 03Instagram is the magazine-on-Instagram. Every post is content Zieda would put her name on. Not "the lounge is open" posts. Real editorial with a real face.
  • 04The two villas are not two brands. They are two floors of the same shopfront. Upkeep on one, permanent change on the other. Same customer, different stage.

The content model is not "marketing around the business." It is: the business IS the content.

The architectural rule

Instapracto and Zenoti are NOT the first layer. They are the LAST layer. They are purpose-built operational and clinical systems — good at that. They sit downstream. The unified CRM + CDP sits upstream. When an appointment is confirmed, the CRM pushes the record into Instapracto or Zenoti via two-way API sync. This is the integration Zieda has been trying to do backwards for three years. It only works in this order.

AI agents live in the automation layer — not as a gimmick, as the only way to hit sub-60-second first-response time across WhatsApp, Instagram DM, web chat and off-hours traffic, without hiring a night shift. Workflows cover the three things marketing exists for: acquisition, retention, engagement.

B.3 · Channels

Centralise channels. Stay consistent. Don't burn out.

The single biggest marketing mistake right now is fragmentation everywhere — three SEO programs, three social calendars, two ad accounts, four agencies, zero coordination. Fix it by collapsing to one of each.

Unified Search

One domain, one SEO program, one content calendar, one keyword universe. Own the ME beauty category in 2–3 quarters.

Unified Social

One Instagram (Zieda's professional magazine), one TikTok, one YouTube. Sub-handles only if they earn their keep. No more @ziedasclinic.dental fragmenting reach.

Press (keep active)

Emirates Woman, L'Officiel, Gulf News, trade publications. The story is already working — keep feeding it. One PR track, not three.

UGC (activate)

Reddit, Quora, beauty forums, review ecosystems. Active seeding, review-response programme, customer story programme. Community-led, not agency-led.

Consistency over intensity

The Sept 2024 – May 2025 $1M brand push worked (sales +50–60%) and then flatlined the moment it stopped. That is the exact shape of a burnout marketing programme. A smaller engine running for 24 months beats a bigger engine running for 8 months and dying.

Stay on. Stay consistent. Don't burn out.

C · Next Steps
As the CMO in the room

When I step into the CMO shoes: this is not a data problem, and it is not a tech problem. It is an absence-of-program problem.

C.1 · The real diagnosis

The team isn't failing. There is no program to fail at.

Looking at Zieda honestly: there isn't a marketing team that's failing. There is no marketing program — nothing has been designed. The body of knowledge about marketing lives entirely in Zieda's head. Everything else has been outsourced in narrow slices (salon agency here, clinic agency there, dental agency nowhere) so nothing talks to anything. It is a shapeless marketing team, not a broken one.

You cannot fix this by adding more tools, more data, more agencies, or more dashboards. You fix it by designing a marketing program — one with a shape, an owner, a cadence, a budget mix, and a scoreboard — and then running it. The tech and data questions become trivial once the program exists, because now there's something for tech and data to serve.

The real unlock is mindset, not infrastructure. Once Zieda stops feeling like marketing is happening to her and starts feeling like she runs it, everything downstream becomes answerable in a week.

C.2 · Operating cadence

Annual + Quarterly operating thoughts. In-house first. Don't feed the agency machine.

Annual
AOP

Revenue targets per line, channel mix, budget allocation, positioning, big bets, a short list of no's. Simple doc, not a 60-page deck.

Quarterly
QOT

What's working, what's not, doubles, kills, owners. The scoreboard for the quarter. The meeting that actually matters.

Monthly
Review

Light. Numbers in, decisions out. No theatre.

On agencies — said as someone who runs one

Agencies will suck the blood out of you and deliver shabby work if you let them be the spine of your marketing team. They are leverage, not substitute. The sequence that works: Zieda → in-house marketing lead → small in-house team (SEO/content, social/community, performance, ops) → agency as on-demand scale, not as the operating brain. Reverse the order and you get exactly what you have today: four agencies, four CRMs, four versions of the brand, and nobody owns the outcome.

Bring the brain in-house. Then hire hands, not heads, outside.
