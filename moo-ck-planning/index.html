Central Kitchen — Production, Dispatch & Procurement Planning

The team playbook: what to make, what to ship each store, and what to reorder — fed by Rista, decided by standard inventory rules. v1 · 21 Jul 2026

The one rule. This tool never writes to Rista. Rista stays the single source of truth. The tool reads Rista + the warehouse, thinks, and suggests — then a human keys the transfer/PO into Rista and we read it back to confirm. read → think → suggest → remind → verify.

This is not a new problem. It's textbook inventory replenishment — par-based (min/max) stock control, plus MRP (turning sales into ingredient demand through the recipe). We use the standard names, not invented ones. There are two domains, and dispatch has two parts.

Domain 1 — Procurement (Vendor → Central)

Reorder raw ingredients from vendors into CEN/CS. Demand = what the kitchen will produce + what the direct-ship goods need restocked, both exploded through the recipe/BOM.

Min (Reorder Point) = daily use × lead time + safety stock Par (Order-up-to) = daily use × (lead time + review gap) + safety stock Inventory position = on-hand (CEN+CS) + on-order (open POs) Order qty = max(0, Par − inventory position)

Grouped by vendor → one reminder = one PO. Uses on-order so we never re-order the same gap twice.

Domain 2 — Dispatch (Central → Stores)

Every morning, decide what each store needs. One formula, two item types:

dispatch = max(0, Par − closing store stock) ← bring up to par + ceil(velocity_7d × buffer_days) ← forward cover + indent (store request) capped by what CEN/CS can supply today

A · Prepared in kitchen

Produce → dispatch

Intermediates the CK makes from raw ingredients — juices (24h), soaked-oat base, granola. Shelf-life bound, so there's a production plan first, then dispatch. A shortfall means "make more today", not "hold stock." This plan is what drives ingredient buying in Domain 1.

B · Direct from warehouse

Ship as-is

Finished/packaged goods bought from vendors and held at Central Store — bottled milk, whole fruit, nut/seed packs, honey. No production. Pure par replenishment. If CS can't cover, it flags "🛒 short — buy" → a demand signal into Procurement.

Known gaps (be honest about these)

  1. BOM is manual & incomplete — Rista's BOM API is closed to us; recipe quantities live in MOO's own recipe book, with some SKUs unmapped. Both domains depend on it → this is the #1 dependency.
  2. Vendor lead time isn't stored anywhere — seed it manually now, derive from PO→GRN history later.
  3. Rista indents are unused — store requests come via WhatsApp; the tool keeps its own indent queue.
  4. Confirm closing store stock reliably reaches Rista daily — the dispatch formula leans on it.
Prior art. Most of this was designed once as the Outpost "Central Kitchen dispatch engine" and parked. The par_levels and dispatch_plans tables already exist (empty) in the warehouse. We resurrect and extend — we don't start over.
