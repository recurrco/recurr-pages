Brief

North star is Pentagram. The ask: an agency site that breaks modern UI rules — no cards, no pills, no soft shadows — and reads like a publication, not a product. Plus a technical layer: mono captions, coordinate markers, instruments, readouts. And a hard commitment: bold black and white, no shades of grey.

Synthesis / Working hypothesis

Editorial publication on a
technical chassis.

Pentagram's confidence + Teenage Engineering's instrument honesty. Case studies read like long-form articles with mono captions, coordinate markers, timestamps, real numbers. Black and white are structural — not mood. Greys banned.

Keep

  • Pentagram-level type scale — hierarchy from size + weight, never from colour-value.
  • Are.na / Teenage Engineering discipline — zero UI ornament, no cards, no pills, no glass.
  • V6 motion vocabulary (matrix rain, braille wave, cursor) as technical substrate — lives under the editorial.
  • Mono captions + coordinate markers as page furniture (REF/01, 2026-04-19, etc).

Kill

  • All greys. Pure black, pure white, one accent maximum.
  • Soft shadows, rounded cards, frosted glass, gradient chrome — the Linear.app vocabulary.
  • Hero headline + sub-headline + CTA templates. Pages are articles, not landing pages.
  • Inline "feature grids" and badge-lists. Specs belong in mono footnotes, not bento boxes.
