Ministry of Oats — Two Programs Internal · Rohit screen-share · May 19, 2026

Two programs of the chain.

Ministry of Oats is a chain. DIY and Oatmeal Gang are programs that sit on top of it. Same brand, same kitchens, same wallet — but they do different work, with different mechanics, for different customers. Here is how each one runs and why they are complementary bets, not competing ones.

The chain spine

MOO is India's premium clean-eating chain. Subway-shaped, Sweetgreen-modeled, India-built. One brand, one menu, one storefront across three surfaces — walk-in · aggregators · ministryofoats.com. Both programs live on that spine.

Program 1

DIY by MOO

Take MOO home. The chain's pantry & merchandising line.

What it is

The ingredients, intermediates, and small-batch products MOO already makes for its own kitchens — packaged, branded, and sold to customers who want to make MOO recipes at home.

Not a CPG company. Not a pivot. A retail extension of the chain that runs on what we already produce.

"We share our recipes. You make them at home. We ship you the ingredients."

How it works

  • Surface ministryofoats.com/shop only. No aggregator. No qcomm. Owned channel, owned data.
  • Payments Wallet-first. Same MOO wallet you use for everything else. Top up, deduct, refill.
  • Production Self-fulfilled, built on the Made From Plants (MFP, JP Nagar) partnership. MFP supplies raw oats and white-labels our oat milk. The 4 in-house SKUs come from MOO kitchens.
  • Brand claim "Small batch." Defensible across all 5 launch SKUs. Sharper than "house-made."
  • Marketing engine The 66-recipe corpus on /diy. Every recipe is a sales page for the ingredients it uses.
  • Frequency Occasional. Top-up cycle. Customers come back when they run out, not on a calendar.

Customer

Existing MOO fans + people who follow our recipes. They already trust the menu; now they want to make it.

Revenue motion

Cart × refill cycle × portfolio expansion. Owned-channel margin (no aggregator commission). LTV grows by adding SKUs to the basket, not by locking the customer into a plan.

Comp set

Yoga Bar's pantry line · Mom's Co retail · the way Sweetgreen sells dressings. Brand-as-ingredient, not a CPG company.

Program 2

Oatmeal Gang

Members of the chain. The loyalty + community + subscription program.

What it is

MOO's members-only program. A weekday breakfast subscription wrapped in a community of run clubs, partnership events, and sampling Sundays — anchored by a Fridge in every gym, club, and apartment we partner with.

Not a rewards program. A real membership where loyalty, predictable revenue, and community live in the same box.

"The best customers ask for more. We bring them closer."

How it works

  • Surface ministryofoats.com (member portal) · walk-in (member benefits) · partner Fridges (the B2B2C edge).
  • Payments Wallet-first. Members load the MOO wallet and the subscription deducts daily. Pause anytime.
  • Programmed cadence A real weekly rhythm — not a coupon book.
  • Partner Gangs Gyms · sports & run clubs · apartments + supermarts. The face of every partnership is a MOO Fridge.
  • Member benefits Discounted subscriptions · sampling events · merch + gifts · fitness events · co-marketing slots.
  • Frequency Recurring. Weekday daily breakfast. Designed to become the morning habit.

The Fridge motion

Every Partner Gang gets a MOO Fridge. Members order via wallet, partner location stocks and serves. The Fridge becomes the brand's face inside someone else's space — a gym, a clubhouse, an apartment lobby.

Customer

MOO power users and regulars. People who are already ordering 2–3 times a week and want the next level of relationship with the brand.

Revenue motion

Subscription × LTV × wallet balance × cross-sell to events. The Gang de-risks monthly revenue — every paying member is a predictable customer for the year.

Comp set

Pret Subscription (depth of loyalty) + Sweetgreen Sweetpass (digital sub mechanic) + a run-club / events layer neither of them has.

Where they meet

One brand, one wallet, two compounding loops.

DIY and the Oatmeal Gang are not separate products — they are two programs of the same chain, sharing the brand, the menu, the kitchens, and most importantly the wallet. A customer can be both a Gang member and a regular DIY shopper, with one balance funding both.

They cover the two non-aggregator levers we need to grow:

  • DIY grows depth of brand — every recipe sold reinforces MOO as the source of clean-eating know-how.
  • Oatmeal Gang grows depth of relationship — every member becomes a predictable, recurring customer with a community gravitating around the brand.
  • Both push the channel mix away from the 95% aggregator dependency that is the central de-risking story for the next 12 months.
