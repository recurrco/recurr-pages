01 / What Press Desk Is

The editorial UI for Recurr Press.

Press Desk lives at recurr.co/press-desk — workflow Kanban plus editorial document lists. The full Sanity Studio is at recurr.co/studio, where every document type and the deep configuration lives.

You'll spend ~95% of your time in /press-desk. Use /studio only when you need to edit site singletons (homepage, contact page) — most editors won't.

The two surfaces

SurfaceURLWhen to use
Press Deskrecurr.co/press-deskDaily editorial work — write, review, schedule, publish
Sanity Studiorecurr.co/studioSite config, careers posts, anything outside Press

What's inside Press Desk

  • My Work — articles assigned to you
  • Articles — All articles · By theme
  • Releases — Weekly drops grouping articles for a coordinated publish
  • Newsletter — Weekly newsletter docs
  • Signals — Signal Engine queue (automated content firehose) + Posted history
  • Library — Themes · Authors (Bylines) · Pillars (reference data)
  • Workflow board — separate tool in left sidebar: Brief → Draft → In Review → Approved Kanban

Every article moves through five states.

Review SLA
In Review by Wednesday for a Friday publish. Pratik clears the review queue mid-week. Don't drop a piece into review at 9pm Thursday and expect Friday.

Starting a new piece

You usually don't. Briefs are seeded ahead of time by Pratik (one per piece in the monthly plan). You pick up an existing Brief from the Kanban.

If you genuinely need to start from scratch:

  1. Sanity Studio → New document → Press Article
  2. Fill in title, slug, excerpt, pillar, byline (yourself), format, theme (current month)
  3. Save → a workflow doc is auto-created in Brief state
  4. Drag yourself to Draft when you start writing

05 / The 5 Pillars

Every article gets exactly one pillar.

Internal classifications — they don't appear as public categories yet. Don't agonize. Pick the closest. Pratik will reassign if needed.

01

Enterprise Software Marketing

The big bucket. B2B SaaS GTM, content marketing, demand gen, PMM, founder-led marketing. Most cold-start SaaStitute pieces fit here.

02

Positioning & Strategy

How companies decide what they are. Categories, frames, pricing, packaging.

03

Design as Communication

Web, product, decks, visual storytelling treated as language, not decoration.

04

Internet & Media

Distribution, content engines, the LLM era, the shift from search to systems-that-influence-buyers. SEO/AEO/GEO pieces live here.

05

Building & Operating

The work itself. Running platforms, ventures, teams. Operational machinery.

Heuristic
Between two pillars? Pick the one the article's closing argument serves. A SEO piece that ends with "and here's how to think about your category" is Positioning & Strategy, not Internet & Media.

07 / Themes & Releases

The umbrella, and the bundle.

Monthly theme

  • One theme per month. Picked by Pratik in the last week of the prior month.
  • Has 4 weekly sub-themes (W1, W2, W3, W4).
  • Every article in that month aligns to the theme.
  • Articles reference the theme via the theme field.
  • Standalone essays (no theme) are allowed. Sujith's first piece had no theme.

Weekly Release

  • Bundles articles that ship together in a given week.
  • Has its own publish time.
  • Status: Building → Scheduled → Published → Reverted.
  • Used for a coordinated drop where 3-4 pieces go live at once.
  • Optional in v1 — pieces can publish independently.

08 / The Image Problem

Currently the system's biggest gap.

Right now there is no clear answer to "what does an image look like on Recurr Press." Pratik is closing this in a parallel design-system thread.

For now

  • Cover required for visual quality, but you can ship without one — listing card looks worse but the page works.
  • Inline images: allowed via the body editor. Add alt text. Add a caption.
  • Pull quotes: preferred over inline imagery for editorial weight.
  • Don't use stock photography. Ever.
  • Diagrams: sketch it and ask Pratik to render it in the house style.

When the design system closes, this section will be replaced with concrete specs.

09 / AI Assist

What it's good at — and what it isn't.

Use AI Assist for

  • Drafting Twitter thread cuts from the body. "Write a 6-tweet thread. Hook first. Question last."
  • Drafting LinkedIn post cuts from the body.
  • Suggesting TLDRs — paste body, ask for 5 options under 160 chars.
  • Excerpt rewriting — when the body's done, regenerate excerpt to match.
  • SEO title variants — 5 keyword-front-loaded options.

Don't use AI Assist for

  • The article body itself. The body is the human's voice. AI ghostwriting kills the brand.
  • Naming pillars or formats (fixed enums — AI gets them wrong).
  • Image generation inside Sanity (use the design pipeline, not Studio AI).
Open thread
AI Assist needs the Sanity Growth plan. Currently on a lower tier. Pratik is closing this. Until then AI features may be unavailable.
10 / Comments & Review

How feedback flows.

Sanity has document-level comments. How we use them:

  • Reviewer opens the doc, reads, leaves comments via the Comments panel on the right.
  • Comments can be on fields ("this excerpt undersells the piece") or on selections in the body (highlight + comment).
  • Author resolves comments by editing OR replying.
  • Once all comments are resolved, the reviewer drags the piece to Approved (Pratik only) or back to Draft (anyone) with a summary comment.
Don't
Comment-and-leave. Always drag the piece to the next state when you're done reviewing.
11 / Publish Checklist

Before hitting Publish.

  • Title — final, not a working title
  • Slug — final (won't change after publish)
  • Excerpt — under 280 chars, makes the click feel earned
  • TLDR — under 160 chars, one sentence
  • Cover image — present (or accept the listing-card hit)
  • Body — proofread end to end
  • Pillar — set
  • Byline / Authors — set
  • Format — set
  • Theme — set (if themed month) or intentionally blank
  • Distribution: Twitter thread, LinkedIn post, newsletter cut written
  • SEO title — only if it differs from editorial title
  • PublishedAt — set to scheduled time

When all green: hit Publish in the upper right of the Studio. Site revalidates within 1–2 seconds via the Sanity webhook → /api/revalidate. No deploy needed.

12 / Common Mistakes

And how to avoid them.

MistakeWhat goes wrongFix
Writing body before brief is locked Half the piece gets thrown away Write the brief into the Excerpt field first. Lock the angle. THEN write the body.
Publishing without a Pillar Listing card looks half-empty; affects SEO Always set Pillar. Required field.
Using Byline AND Authors Old/new authorship UI conflict Solo: use Byline. Multi: use Authors array (leave Byline empty). Site renders Authors if present, else Byline.
Skipping distribution cuts Piece goes live with no social plan Write Twitter + LinkedIn cuts during Draft. Easier when body is fresh.
Editing a published piece's slug Breaks all inbound links Don't. If you must, set up a redirect at the Vercel layer.
Stuck in Brief because no body Pratik can't approve a stub Either fill the body or move it back to Backlog. Don't leave Briefs sitting.
