Recurr Why the gap exists
Why the gap exists

Three gaps. One is structural. Two are fixable fast.

Gap 1 — No third-party evidence

97.8% of LLM citations come from domains Krisatwork doesn't own. There are currently zero relevant mentions of Krisatwork on any page an LLM cites for AI sales assistant prompts. The BriefGlance funding article exists — but funding news does not answer "which tool should I use." It is not being cited for any buying intent prompt.

Why it matters: The model has nothing to draw from except Krisatwork's own site — which barely registers because it ranks for zero keywords.
Gap 2 — No review platform presence

No G2 profile. No Capterra listing. No Product Hunt page. These are anchor points LLMs trust heavily for tool comparisons and "best X" queries. Competitors with even 5–10 G2 reviews appear consistently. Krisatwork has no entry point here.

Who wins because of this: AskElephant (G2 presence + 200+ HubSpot Marketplace installs cited), Gong, Outreach, Sybill.
Gap 3 — Decaying owned content + technical blocker

4 blog posts, last published Sept–Oct 2025. That is 5–6 months of decay — past the threshold where content starts losing LLM relevance. The localhost canonical issue means Google cannot properly index even what exists. And there are no comparison pages, alternatives pages, or use-case pages — the formats LLMs pull from most reliably.

The model interprets content neglect as a signal that the brand doesn't care about accuracy. That is precisely what it's trying to surface.

Recurr The 90-day program
The program

Build the evidence environment in three months.

Two parallel tracks: third-party placement (the pages LLMs already trust) and owned content (the pages LLMs will start trusting). Both compound together.

Month 1 (April) — Fix + Foundation Technical, directory, baseline
  • Fix canonical URL — Remove localhost:3000 canonical, set to krisatwork.com. Submit sitemap. Verify indexing in GSC. This is the single highest-leverage technical fix.
  • G2 profile live — Create profile. Accurate description, all product categories selected, pricing range, 3–5 initial reviews from existing customers. G2 is cited in nearly every LLM roundup response.
  • Capterra + Software Advice listing — Same profile data as G2. These cluster together in LLM citations.
  • Product Hunt launch brief — Prepare the launch page, tagline, and hunter outreach. Do not launch until Month 2 content is live (LLMs should find something when they follow the link).
  • Baseline audit run — Run all 15 prompts manually across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini. Record: Krisatwork mentioned (Y/N), which competitors appear, which third-party URLs are cited. This is the Week 1 baseline.
  • Content brief × 4 articles — Write briefs for the four highest-priority pieces (see content plan below). Briefs ready by end of Month 1.
Month 2 (May) — Content + Third-Party Placement Publish, place, pitch
  • Publish Article 1: "Why your sales reps spend 70% of their time on non-selling work — and what to do about it." Target: B3 prompt cluster. AEO structure: clear definition, comparison table, FAQ block, author attribution. Fresh content as of May 2026.
  • Publish Article 2: "AI sales companion vs AI sales assistant — what's the difference and which one solves the real problem." Target: C4 prompt. This is the framing article that defines Krisatwork's differentiated category and owns the positioning.
  • Publish Article 3: "How to reduce SaaS fatigue for revenue teams — and why a unified AI companion beats adding another point solution." Target: B5, A4, A5 prompts. This is the open territory prompt cluster. No one owns it. Move fast.
  • Publish Article 4: "Krisatwork vs Gong — which one is right for your team?" Comparison page. Target: C1 prompt. Honest framing (Gong = coaching analytics, Krisatwork = unified companion). LLMs pull heavily from comparison pages.
  • Roundup outreach — 3 targets: Pitch goconsensus.com, guideflow.com, and cirrusinsight.com for inclusion in their AI sales assistant roundups. Provide a 100-word product summary, screenshot, and pricing for their editorial team. These three pages are being cited actively.
  • Product Hunt launch — Launch with warm upvote pool. Aim for Top 5 of day. Product Hunt pages are crawled and cited by LLMs for "new AI tools" and category searches.
  • Mid-month audit check — Rerun 5 highest-priority prompts (A1, A2, B1, B2, B5). Record any movement. Adjust outreach priorities if needed.
Month 3 (June) — Authority + Compound Reviews, more placements, monitoring cadence live
  • G2 reviews campaign — 10+ verified reviews from customers. Work with the Krisatwork CS team. G2 profile with 10+ reviews ranks higher in LLM category responses than one with 0–3. Reviews also surface specific use cases the model can cite.
  • Publish Article 5: "How Krisatwork helped [Customer] reduce sales admin by X% and close deals faster." A structured case study — specific numbers, named customer (if approved), before/after workflow. LLMs cite case studies for commercial intent prompts.
  • Publish Article 6: "Best AI sales assistant alternatives to Gong for mid-market teams." Alternatives page. Target: C1, C2 prompts. Feature Krisatwork prominently. Also covers Sybill, Read.ai, Avoma — balanced framing so it earns third-party trust.
  • Second-tier roundup outreach — 3 more targets: 11x.ai blog, nooks.ai blog, and one independent newsletter (Sales Hacker / RevGenius). These have high LLM citation frequency in the category.
  • HubSpot / Salesforce marketplace listing — If not live, create. AskElephant explicitly cites "200+ HubSpot Marketplace installs" — that is a trust signal LLMs pick up from third-party coverage. Marketplace listings also generate backlinks and citation mentions.
  • Full monthly audit report — Run all 15 prompts across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini. Compare to Week 1 baseline. Measure: prompts where Krisatwork appears (target: 4–6 of 15), sentiment (recommended vs mentioned), which third-party pages are now cited.
  • Month 4–6 content brief + third-party roadmap — Plan the next cycle based on what moved and what didn't.

Content architecture — AEO structure for every article

Every piece built to this spec so LLMs can extract and cite confidently.

Required elements

✓ Clear entity definition in first 150 words
✓ Comparison table with competitors
✓ FAQ block (3–5 Q&As, answers in 2–3 sentences)
✓ Author page with credentials linked
✓ Schema markup (Article, FAQPage, Product)
✓ Publication + last-updated date visible
✓ Internal links to all other GEO content pieces

Refresh schedule

Every article reviewed and updated at 90-day mark.
Trigger for early refresh: if article drops out of LLM citation after appearing, or if a competitor publishes fresher content on the same topic.
B2B blog content has a ~5 month shelf life before LLM decay begins. Build the refresh into the program, not as an afterthought.
