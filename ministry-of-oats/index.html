Why We Exist

Cereals disappeared from Indian adulthood.

When we all grew up in India — every Gen Z, every millennial — we had cereals as kids. Chocos, cornflakes, whatever our parents bought. Then somewhere during teenage and beyond, it just disappeared.

We were told to "eat proper food." Breakfast became parathas, idlis, or nothing at all. Growing up meant giving up the fun stuff.

We're bringing cereals back. Not cornflakes. Not the sugary stuff. Real, functional, delicious cereals — overnight oats, porridge bowls, chia puddings — that are high in fibre, high in protein, and actually taste incredible.

Because growing up doesn't mean growing boring. Eating cereals is a way of rebelling against adulthood — letting the kid in you live a little more.
