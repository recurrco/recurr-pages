Why the chat box is the new homepage.

Interface, workflow, data, business model. Each layer of the SaaS stack dies on its own clock — but the interface goes first, and most teams are still optimizing the layer the asteroid has already passed.

Byline — Pratik Mishra Published 2026-04-22 08 min read Filed — Positioning · AI · New Stack Share / Listen / Save →
Chapter / 01 Interface
Reading time 08 min Series 4 essays · 1 argument Theme May 2026

The interface layer is the first place an AI-native shift becomes visible — and the first place an incumbent loses ground without realising it. For a decade, the homepage was a navigation surface: hero, nav bar, three feature tiles, a pricing link. The user came with intent, hunted, found, clicked. The interface served retrieval.

That contract is breaking. The new entry point is conversational — a chat box, a prompt field, a single line that promises to compose what the user wants instead of pointing them toward it. Buyers no longer hunt across menus. They ask. The interface is collapsing into a sentence.

When the interface is a sentence, your homepage isn't a hero — it's a system prompt.
— Pull quote / Chapter 01

Most product teams are still solving for the old contract. They are A/B-testing nav-bar copy and tweaking pricing-page hierarchies while the locus of the buyer's first contact has already moved upstream — into a model, into a chat, into a context window where the homepage is a few hundred tokens of priming, not a layout.

What changes when this is true? The brand becomes the system prompt. The product becomes the call. The website becomes the documentation surface that answers a model's clarification questions, not a marketing artefact for a human reader. The job of the homepage shifts from convincing a buyer to teaching a model how to recommend you.

What dies on this layer.

Three things. The first is the navigation paradigm itself — top-nav, mega-menus, the scroll-and-scan. The second is hero-page rhetoric — the four-word promise paired with a smiling stock photo. The third, less obvious, is the SEO-led content site as a primary acquisition surface. When the model is the discovery layer, model-readability replaces page rank as the selection mechanism.

None of these die instantly. Interface change rarely does. They die in a long tail of irrelevance: the analytics keep showing visits, but the visits stop converting, and the visits that do convert come pre-qualified by a model the marketing team never integrated with. By the time the dashboard turns red, the buyer journey has already routed around you.

The thing to build instead.

A homepage that earns the recommendation. Less rhetoric, more answer. Fewer features, more facts. A press surface that does the convincing in long form. A team page that reads like a masthead. A datasheet that explains the operating system, not the value prop. The site stops being a sales asset and starts being the documentation a curious model would cite.

In the next chapter we go one layer deeper — into workflow. The interface change is loud and visible. The workflow change is quieter, and bigger.
