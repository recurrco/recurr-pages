Building a demand engine for TNT's plywood business

1. What We Sell

TNT (Tone & Timber) is a plywood and board trading business based in Bangalore. We buy from manufacturers (primarily from Patna and other sources) and sell in the Bangalore market. We operate from a depot in North Bangalore.

FY25-26 numbers: ~3.73 Cr sales, 321 invoices, 56 customers, 11 vendors. Net profit: ~14L.

The margin story: Our highest-margin products are Commercial Plywood (MR grade), HMR boards, PVC boards, and FJB. The BWR/BWP grades (moisture/marine) are competitive and sold primarily for bundling — they pull customers in but don't carry fat margins. The real money is in the commodity grades sold at unmatched pricing thanks to our direct sourcing from Grace (Multyply Industries, Patna).

Pricing Advantage

We also offer bulk pricing which drops rates by Rs 1–5/sft further. On commercial ply, our rates are described internally as "unmatched pricing — lots of margin to beat." This is because we source directly from the manufacturer (Multyply Industries, our 68% supplier), cutting out middlemen.

Vendor Concentration

One risk to note: 68% of our purchases come from one vendor (Multyply Industries, Patna). This gives us pricing power but creates supply chain dependency. We have 10 other vendors for specialty products.

2. Our Buyer Types

We have three distinct buyer segments, each with different buying behaviour, volumes, and economics.

A. Resellers & Dealers (Ruchir's channel)

Who: Timber depots, plywood shops, and small dealers across Bangalore who buy in bulk and resell to their walk-in customers.

Volume: Our largest revenue channel today. Top 5 customers are all resellers and they account for ~48% of revenue.

Behaviour: Repeat, relationship-driven, cash/credit. Weekly purchases. Price-sensitive — they make money on the spread between our rates and their retail rates.

Why they buy from us: Our commercial ply pricing is unbeatable in Bangalore. They can't get Grace brand MR-grade plywood cheaper elsewhere.

Payment: Mix of cash and credit. Some large resellers run 15–30 day credit cycles.

B. Applied Industries (Pratik's channel — the exciting one)

Who: Companies that convert plywood into value-added products. They're NOT selling plywood — they're using it as raw material for something else.

Volume: Early stage. One active customer (Pronk). But each customer, once onboarded, orders in very large volumes with predictable repeat cycles.

Behaviour: Structured procurement — POs, invoices, banking payments. Much more professional than reseller cash trade.

Why they buy from us: For one-time-use applications (crates, shuttering, stalls), quality doesn't matter — cost is everything. Our commercial ply is the cheapest available.

Payment: Invoice-based, 15–30 day terms. Proper banking relationships.

More on this below — this is where the demand engine opportunity is.

C. Architects & Individual Buyers

Who: Interior designers, architects, and individual homeowners doing renovation or construction.

Volume: Smallest segment. Occasional, project-based purchases.

Behaviour: One-off or short-term. Influenced by brand names and specifications. Higher-grade products (BWR, BWP, HDHMR) sell better here.

Why they buy from us: Competitive pricing + local depot pickup. Not a focus area for growth.

3. Applied Industries — Why This Is Exciting

The thesis: Applied industries are companies that consume plywood as raw material. They don't care about brand or aesthetics — they care about cost per sheet. Our commercial MR-grade plywood is the cheapest in Bangalore because we source directly. Every applied industry customer is a high-volume, repeating, invoice-paying account. This is the segment where a demand engine can move the needle.

Why Applied > Resellers for growth

  • Larger order volumes per customer — a packaging company orders truckloads, not bundles
  • Better payment cycles — invoiced, banked, structured. No cash-chase.
  • More defensible — once you're in a procurement system, you stay. Switching costs are real.
  • Higher predictability — repeat orders on a cycle. You can forecast.
  • Institutional trust — proper buyer-seller relationships, not personal favours.

The Five Applied Industry Segments

1. Packaging (Priority #1)

What they do: Build industrial wooden crates, pallets, and boxes for shipping heavy/oversized/high-value cargo — aerospace parts, machinery, turbines, auto components.

Why cost matters: The packaging is one-time use. It gets discarded after the cargo reaches its destination. Nobody cares about finish or durability — they need the cheapest sheet that holds structural integrity for one journey.

Our first customer: Pronk (Pronk Multiservice India Pvt Ltd) — subsidiary of Tri-Wall Japan, Electronic City Phase 2, Bangalore. 11+ years. Serves aerospace, renewable energy, railways, defence, FMCG, oil & gas.

More prospects in Bangalore: BangalorePack, HMS Wooden Box (41 years!), Rish Pack, A R Packers, LR Packing, Sulaksh Packaging, SSIC Bangalore. We have a list of 8 qualified prospects.

Why exciting: India's manufacturing and export growth is accelerating. Every piece of heavy machinery shipped out of Bangalore needs a wooden crate. This segment grows with India's industrial output.

2. Shuttering / Formwork

What they do: Build temporary wooden forms into which concrete is poured during construction. The plywood is used 5–6 times and then discarded.

Why cost matters: High volume, short lifecycle. Builders care about Rs/use. Our commercial ply at Rs 20/sft gives them ~Rs 3.5/use — cheaper than any competitor.

Buyers: Construction companies, formwork rental companies. Massive volume segment — the entire Bangalore construction industry is a buyer.

3. Furniture OEM

What they do: Manufacture furniture at scale. They buy plywood as raw material for back panels, drawer bottoms, internal structural frames — parts you never see.

Why cost matters: Hidden parts don't need premium material. Commercial ply is ideal for structural/invisible components in mass-manufactured furniture.

Buyers: Range from small workshops to large manufacturers. More diverse, harder to crack at scale, but sticky once in.

4. Exhibition & Events

What they do: Build temporary stalls, booths, display structures for trade shows, exhibitions, corporate events. Built once, used for 3–5 days, then dismantled.

Why cost matters: Pure disposable use. Bangalore has a dense trade show calendar (BIEC, Palace Grounds, etc.).

Buyers: Event fabrication companies. Seasonal but can be high-volume during peak exhibition months.

5. Signage

What they do: Build temporary/semi-permanent signboards and display boards. Painted or laminated commercial ply for shop signs, construction hoarding, political banners.

Why cost matters: Low-cost material for short-lifespan applications.

Buyers: Signage fabricators, printing companies, construction companies (hoarding).

The Common Thread

Every applied industry has the same buying logic: they need the cheapest plywood available because it's going to be used once (or a few times) and discarded. They don't care about brand, face veneer, or moisture resistance. They care about Rs/sft and structural integrity. That's exactly what we sell — commercial MR-grade plywood at the lowest rates in Bangalore.

Market Context

  • India plywood market: Rs 248 Bn (2025) growing to Rs 392 Bn (2034) at 5.2% CAGR
  • Residential = 55% of consumption, but construction and packaging are the fast-growing industrial segments
  • Organized sector = 62% — but applied industries often buy from unorganized traders. We can be the organized, reliable alternative.
