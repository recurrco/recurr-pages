Why this is different

What is the semantic layer?

The version of your marketing data your team actually trusts. 50+ connectors — every channel, attribution platform, and your warehouse — all joined at deepest granularity inside Clarisights, expressed in your team's metric definitions, governed by your workspace's ACL. Connectors hand the AI raw fields. The semantic layer hands it answers.

01

Cross-channel by default.

Every channel, attribution platform, and your data warehouse — 50+ connectors — joined at deepest granularity inside Clarisights before the AI sees the query.

The AI doesn't stitch numbers from four API calls. It asks one question against one semantic layer. Cross-channel CPA, blended ROAS, MER arrive as single columns. Not math problems.

Your KPIs, not raw API fields.

Setup

Three steps to connect. One step to personalize.

Existing Clarisights customers can enable the MCP from their workspace. There's nothing new to provision and no separate tokens to manage. The AI client signs in with the user's own Clarisights credentials.

01

Admin turns on AI access.

Workspace admin flips the global toggle at Settings → Workspace → AI access.

Settings → Workspace → AI access
02

Open MCP settings in your account.

Sign in to Clarisights and visit your MCP settings page to retrieve the connection URL for your workspace.

clarisights.com/dashboard/mcp-settings
03

Add the MCP to your AI tool.

Paste the URL into Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, Gemini, or any MCP-compatible client. Sign in with your Clarisights account. Your existing ACL applies.

Optional · personalize
04

Add a SKILL.md for team context.

The MCP exposes vanilla tools. Domain and team context come through your AI client. Pair the MCP with a SKILL.md (template provided) to teach the AI your team's vocabulary, naming conventions, and default lenses.

Frequently asked

Questions, answered.

An MCP (Model Context Protocol) server that connects Clarisights to any MCP-compatible AI tool: Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, Gemini. The AI gets six tools it can call on its own to discover your data sources, query your metrics, and read your reports. Every call respects your team's Clarisights permissions.

Think of it as the analyst interface for your marketing data, exposed to whichever AI client your team has already adopted.

Adjacent MCPs are connectors-first. They move data from many sources and expose a query API on top. Clarisights' MCP exposes a semantic layer your team has been building for years: custom KPIs, governed dimensions, saved cross-channel segments, dashboards. The AI talks in your team's vocabulary on day one, governed by your workspace ACL. That's the difference.

It also scales further. The MCP is designed for programmatic teams running every channel with a full custom-metric library and complex account groupings, not five-source setups.

Anything that speaks MCP. Claude, ChatGPT (with the connector framework), Cursor, Gemini, and the rest of the growing MCP ecosystem. If a client supports custom MCP servers, the Marketing Data MCP works there.

Every channel and source connected to your Clarisights workspace — 50+ connectors across ads, analytics, attribution, CRM, and commerce. The full list lives on the sources and integrations pages. Custom report segments you've saved in Clarisights are addressable too.

If it's in your Clarisights workspace, the MCP can see it.

Yes. Custom metrics are surfaced first by the list_metrics tool, ranked by your team's usage. Custom dimensions, saved segments, account groupings, and dashboard structures are all addressable through the MCP's discovery surface.

The MCP exposes the semantic layer your team has already curated. That's the difference from connectors-first MCPs that hand the AI raw API fields.

As far back as your data goes in Clarisights.

Every MCP query is bound by the same ACL as the logged-in Clarisights user. There are no separate tokens to manage. Admins control AI access through a single global toggle at Workspace → Settings → AI access.

Revoke a user's Clarisights access and every downstream AI client loses access on the next request.

The Marketing Data MCP is a server, not an AI model. Data leaves Clarisights only in response to a user's prompt, returned to that user's AI client. Whatever data-use policy you've negotiated with your AI vendor (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google) applies on their side. That contract is between your team and them.

Clarisights does not retain user-level query data, and never trains anything.

Enterprise-grade limits out of the box. We've sized them so a heavy analyst-mode user runs comfortably without thinking about caps.

The MCP is included on every Clarisights plan. It's not an add-on, and there's no tier restriction. Currently rolling out in beta to existing customers. New teams can see it through a demo.

Read-only today. Write operations are on the roadmap: creating analyses, drafting reports, generating summaries inside Clarisights, plus admin operations like updating custom dimensions through the AI. We're shipping read first because we want every answer trusted before we let the AI write.
