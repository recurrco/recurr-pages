Section / 01

Three principles.

The product rests on three pillars. Each is load-bearing.
Principle / 01
Data

Any data in, any data out.

One data model carries paid, organic, app, web, product catalog, competitive, AI search, and affiliates. Built flexible so new channels slot in as they arrive.

Inside Clarisights, the model powers LTV curves, payback periods, cohort analysis, modeled revenue, and variable payouts natively. No configuration, no joins on the fly.

Outside Clarisights, the same model is your measurement team's source of truth. Pre-harmonised, versioned, incrementally updated. It feeds an MMM or an incrementality test without anyone building a pipeline.

Data in from everywhere. Intelligence out to everyone who needs it.

Principle / 02
Intelligence

Intelligence, not just reporting.

Every question a performance team asks cuts across sources. Clarisights answers across sources natively.

Your product catalog is a first-class analytical dimension. Which categories win on which channels. Which creative formats convert for which segments. Where the catalog has zero ad coverage.

Search is a unified surface. Paid, organic, competitive, and AI search visibility in one view. See cannibalisation. Find gaps. Track how the brand appears in LLM-generated answers next to the traditional rankings.

Catalog and search live in the data model, not on top of a dashboard.

Principle / 03
Surface

Built for humans and machines.

Clarisights treats your team and your AI as equal users of the same surface.

For practitioners. Reports are documents. A blank canvas — tables, charts, annotations, text on one page. Collaboration inline, next to the data. Clean for a first-time user; flexible for a power user building custom cohort windows and modeled coefficients.

For machines. The data model, the report structure, the UI — all readable by an LLM. Through the Model Context Protocol, your harmonised cross-channel metrics are available from Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini.

Build a report on Monday. Ask your AI to extend it on Tuesday.
Query DSL · Creative Score
{:type  "totals"
 :query [
   [:sources   "creative_performance"]
   [:aggregate]
   [:select    "concept" "country"]
   [[:add
     [:multiply [:divide "ctr"       4.0] 0.30]
     [:multiply [:divide 1.0        "cpm"] 0.20]
     [:multiply [:divide "hook_rate" 100] 0.30]
     [:multiply [:divide 1.0        "cpa"] 0.20]]
    :as "creative_score"]]}

Section / 02

Questions the canvas answers.

The ones that used to need a data-team ticket · A representative six
  • Q / 01 Cohort · Payback How long until this cohort pays back — and how does that change if we move $500K into CTV? Answered inline
  • Q / 02 Paid × Organic Is paid search cannibalising our organic brand rankings, and by how much per query cluster? Unified search
  • Q / 03 Catalog × Channel Which product categories win on which channels — and where does our catalog have zero ad coverage? Catalog dimension
  • Q / 04 AI Search How does our brand appear in LLM-generated answers, and against which competitors? Native tracking
  • Q / 05 Creative × Velocity Is this creative format fatiguing, or is our audience saturating? Same question, two very different answers. Segment × velocity
  • Q / 06 AI · MCP Can my AI assistant extend last week's report, pull fresh data, and post the summary to Slack — without leaving the chat? Yes

How the launch goes to market · For Apr 22 review · Confidential
Layered launch

Warmest audience first, then pruned nurture, then a selective amplifier.

Each play compounds on the one before. No broadcasts.
Play / 01

Seed it with every current customer.

Every existing customer hears this first. Exec briefing, early-access credentials, CSM-led walk-through. Their teams are the highest-signal cohort — they find the edges, and their quotes shape the first-party narrative.
