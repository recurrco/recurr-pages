02 — Three Acquisition Layers

Editorial + Build + Aggregation

Every piece of content we produce falls into one of three layers. Each has different effort, speed, and compounding characteristics.

Layer 1 — Editorial

Deep. Human. Compounds.

Essays, research posts, co-authored pieces, podcast, newsletter. High effort, slow to build, but creates lasting authority. This is where credibility lives.

Layer 2 — Build

Programmatic. Scalable.

pSEO projects, directories, tools, trackers, research databases, interactive experiences. Medium effort, many pages per project. Each one ranks + demonstrates capability + generates leads.

Layer 3 — Aggregation

Automated. Daily. Fast.

Signal Engine: auto-ingest from sources, tag, score, publish. Low effort after setup. Feeds Twitter daily, accumulates into a living intelligence database on the website.

05 — Signal Engine

The aggregation machine

Autonomous content collection, scoring, and publishing. Human review for catastrophe prevention only.

INGEST (autonomous, daily) Sources: RSS, Twitter lists, newsletters, HN, Product Hunt, funding databases, Google Alerts Tag by topic + entity (company, person, category) Score: relevance x novelty QUEUE (Pratik batch-approves) Scan, kill anything off, approve batch OUTPUT 1: Twitter @recurrco Daily signals. 3-10 per day. Signal + one-line take. OUTPUT 2: Website accumulation page Living database. Trends over time. Company movements. Category shifts. Like a stock exchange for B2B marketing/SaaS moves. Gets more valuable every day. Compounds. SEO magnet.

The virtuous loop: Signal Engine collects daily. Twitter publishes fragments (acquisition). Website accumulates the database (authority + SEO). Database informs editorial (essay topics). Editorial drives inbound (clients).

Monthly themes,
weekly sprints

  • Reddit engagement follows theme
  • Twitter signals weighted toward theme

  • Monday: Sprint planning. Review items, confirm owners, flag gaps.
  • Wednesday: Review queue cleared. Pratik approves anything in review.
  • Friday: Week close. Everything published or pushed with reason.
  • Review SLA: -2 days from publish date
07 — Content Lifecycle

From idea to live

Backlog → Todo → In Progress → In Review → Approved → Done

Pratik is Editor-in-Chief. Nothing publishes without his approval. Every piece moves through review before going live.

Review SLA: Draft must be in review 2 days before publish date. If publishing Friday, review by Wednesday.

08 — Voice

How we sound

Opinionated, not neutral. We take positions. We name uncomfortable truths.

Framework-first. Every piece leaves the reader with a mental model they can use.

Practitioner tone. Written by people who do the work, not people who observe it.

Generous. Give away the thinking. Don't gatekeep.

No jargon performance. Plain language about hard ideas.

09 — Authorship

Named people,
not a logo

Named individuals, multiple per piece. "Pratik and Sujith" style, not "Recurr."

Research pieces: "Recurr" team byline.

Claude as credited co-author. We don't hide it.

Guest operators as authors. Influential people with built-in distribution. When someone co-authors, they share on social. Free distribution.

Author section is a first-class design element, not a footnote. Each author's personal property (Twitter, LinkedIn) is a marketing asset. The press distributes through people, not just the website.
