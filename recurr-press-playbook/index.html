02 — Three Acquisition Layers

Editorial + Builds + Aggregation

Every piece of content we produce falls into one of three layers. Each has different effort, speed, and compounding characteristics. The goal across all three: acquisition.

Layer 1 — Editorial

Deep. Human. Compounds.

Essays, research posts, co-authored pieces, podcast, newsletter. High effort, slow to build, but creates lasting authority. This is where credibility lives.

Layer 2 — Builds

Programmatic. Interactive. Scalable.

What Pratik and Claude make together for scale and interaction. Calculators, quizzes, automated audits, interactive tools, directories, research databases, skills. Artifacts that rank, demonstrate capability, and generate leads. Each build is an acquisition engine in itself. These also serve as a living repository for the @recurrco Twitter aggregation layer — the data and intelligence these builds collect feeds the daily signal stream.

Layer 3 — Aggregation

Automated. Daily. Fast.

Signal Engine: auto-ingest from sources, tag, score, publish. Low effort after setup. Feeds Twitter daily, accumulates into a living intelligence database on the website.

03 — Distribution Architecture

Where content lives

We're building a channel-first distribution play. Instead of creating content and then figuring out where to put it, we've picked the channels that matter most and built native strategies for each. Every channel has a distinct role, a distinct format, and a distinct reason it exists in our system. The website ties everything together as the master index.

The master content index

recurr.co/press

Everything we ever publish — every essay, every research post, every build project, every podcast episode, every newsletter issue — is linked from the website. This is the single source of truth for all Recurr Press output. If it doesn't exist on recurr.co, it doesn't exist.

The website is not just a blog. It's the content index: a structured, searchable, ever-growing library of our thinking. Every channel we operate feeds back here. Podcast episodes get show notes pages. Newsletter editions get web mirrors. Build projects live here natively. Twitter signals accumulate into a living database page.

This is also where SEO compounds. Every piece of editorial, every programmatic build, every aggregated signal — they all create pages that rank. The website is the gravity well that pulls everything together.

Primary — Home Base

Video-first podcast

YouTube Channel

YouTube is our dedicated channel for the podcast — video-first, long-form conversations with operators, founders, and practitioners. One guest per week, 30-45 minutes, always recorded on video.

Why YouTube specifically: It's the only platform where long-form video content compounds over time. A podcast episode uploaded today gets discovered by new viewers months and years later through search and recommendations. Unlike audio-only podcasts that live behind app walls, YouTube episodes are indexable, embeddable, and shareable.

Distribution-first strategy: Every guest we invite has a built-in audience. When they share the episode, they bring their network to our channel. We're not building an audience from zero — we're borrowing distribution from every guest, every week.

Episodes are mirrored as show notes pages on the website with key takeaways, timestamps, and related reading.

Weekly — Podcast

The aggregation machine

@recurrco

Twitter is where our Signal Engine lives — the automated aggregation layer that runs daily. We ingest signals from across the web (RSS feeds, Twitter lists, newsletters, Hacker News, Product Hunt, funding databases, Google Alerts), tag them by topic and entity, score for relevance and novelty, and publish 3-10 signals per day with a one-line take.

Why Twitter for aggregation: Twitter's interest-graph beats geography. LinkedIn shows your content to people nearby. Twitter shows it to people who care about the same things. For a B2B marketing and design company, this is a massive advantage. We reach the right people regardless of where they sit.

The builds from Layer 2 — our calculators, audit tools, directories, research databases — also serve as the repository that feeds this channel. The data and intelligence these artifacts collect becomes raw material for daily signals. The build layer and the aggregation layer are connected: builds collect, Twitter distributes.

The virtuous loop: Signal Engine collects daily → Twitter publishes fragments (acquisition) → Website accumulates the database (authority + SEO) → Database informs editorial (essay topics) → Editorial drives inbound (clients).

Automated — Daily Signals

Weekly newsletter

Substack

The newsletter is our retention channel. While every other channel is about acquisition — finding new people and bringing them in — the newsletter keeps the people who already care close. One drop per week, directly in their inbox.

What it is: Mental models for venture businesses. Not tactical "5 tips for better email subject lines" content. This is about how to think about marketing, design, building, and growing ventures. Frameworks that stick. The kind of writing people forward to their co-founders.

Why Substack: It has a built-in discovery network. New subscribers find you through recommendations from other Substack writers. It's also the cleanest reading experience for long-form writing — no ads, no algorithmic feed, just the essay in their inbox. And the archive becomes a searchable library on Substack's domain, adding another SEO surface.

Weekly — Retention

Team-driven engagement

Team handles (personal accounts)

Reddit is our research and distribution channel, operated through team members' personal accounts. We don't have a Recurr subreddit. We never will. The strategy is to embed in existing communities where our audience already lives — marketing subreddits, SaaS communities, startup forums, design discussions.

Why personal handles: Reddit is anti-brand. Corporate accounts get downvoted. Personal accounts that contribute genuine value get upvoted. Our team members engage as practitioners sharing real experience, not as a company pushing content. This is identical to the LinkedIn principle — people trust people, not logos — but on Reddit, this rule is enforced by the community itself.

How it works: Each week, the team engages in subreddits aligned to the monthly theme. Answer questions. Share frameworks. Link to relevant Recurr Press pieces only when genuinely useful. Reddit engagement is both research (we learn what people actually struggle with) and distribution (well-received answers drive traffic back to the website).

Team Effort — Research + Distribution

Why we're not investing here

Personal profiles only

LinkedIn is deliberately not a focus channel for Recurr Press. Here's why.

Geography-bound algorithm: LinkedIn's feed algorithm is geography-first. It shows your content to people in your city, your connections, your industry cluster. For a company building a global interest-graph play (via Twitter and SEO), this is a fundamental mismatch. LinkedIn reach is local; our audience is global.

Company pages are dead: LinkedIn company pages get a fraction of the reach that personal profiles do. The platform has systematically deprioritized company content in favor of individual posts. Fighting this is a losing battle.

What we do instead: Team members repurpose Press content on their personal profiles. Pratik, Sujith, Ankita — each person shares relevant pieces through their own lens, in their own voice. This is the same principle as Reddit: people trust people, not logos. Personal handles are the best marketing tools on both platforms.

The Recurr company page gets low-effort maintenance. Cover photo, about section, occasional share. That's it. The real LinkedIn strategy is the team's individual presence.

05 — Editorial Guidelines

How we operate,
how we sound

Operating Rhythm

Monthly themes, weekly sprints.

  • Pratik picks theme for next month
  • Theme broken into 4 weekly sub-themes
  • All content streams align to theme
  • Reddit engagement follows theme
  • Twitter signals weighted toward theme
  • 1 Build project ships per month
  • 1 Research artifact ships per month
  • Theme set by 15th of prior month

Voice

Opinionated, not neutral. We take positions. We name uncomfortable truths.

Framework-first. Every piece leaves the reader with a mental model they can use.

Practitioner tone. Written by people who do the work, not people who observe it.

Generous. Give away the thinking. Don't gatekeep.

No jargon performance. Plain language about hard ideas.

Content Lifecycle

Backlog → Todo → In Progress → In Review → Approved → Done

Pratik is the editorial lead — ultimately responsible and accountable for the entire editorial pipeline. This is his primary KRA.

Review SLA: Draft in review 2 days before publish date.

Tracked in Outwork (outwork.recurr.co). Weekly cycles. Channel modules.

Weekly Sprint

  • Monday: Sprint planning. Review items, confirm owners, flag gaps.
  • Wednesday: Review queue cleared. Pratik approves anything in review.
  • Friday: Week close. Everything published or pushed with reason.
  • Review SLA: -2 days from publish date

Authorship

Named individuals, multiple per piece. "Pratik and Sujith" style, not "Recurr."

Research pieces: "Recurr" team byline.

Claude as credited co-author. We don't hide it.

Guest operators as authors. Influential people with built-in distribution. When someone co-authors, they share on social. Free distribution.

Author section is a first-class design element, not a footnote. Each author's personal property (Twitter, LinkedIn) is a marketing asset.

Decisions Locked

  • Monthly themes drive all content. Pratik picks. Team aligns.
  • Two external faces per week. Podcast + co-authored piece.
  • Twitter = automated Signal Engine. Interest-graph beats geography.
  • Reddit = team research + distribution. Never build a subreddit.
  • LinkedIn = personal profiles only. Company page is low-effort.
  • No case narratives. Show thinking, not client work.
  • Pillars are internal compass. Not public categories. Formalize after 50 articles.
