What typefaces does recurr use? +
Serif (New York / Georgia) is the primary voice for all editorial content — headlines, body, pull quotes, article text. Monospace (SF Mono / Menlo) handles metadata, labels, code blocks, and marginalia annotations. Sans-serif (SF Pro / system-ui) is reserved for navigation and UI chrome only.
Why cool blue-green grays instead of warm cream? +
Warm cream felt cozy but lacked crispness. The cool blue-green tint sits between paper and screen — grounded enough for long reading sessions, sharp enough for professional presentation. It pairs better with the marker accents, creating contrast without collision.
How do the marker colors map to content types? +
Red marks emphasis — underlines, warnings, and important callouts. Yellow marks insight — highlights, key phrases, and "aha" moments. Blue marks navigation — links, interactive elements, and CTAs. Green marks status — active, live, success states. This mapping is consistent across all pages.
What's the motion philosophy? +
Three layers: ASCII self-draw on scroll (signature, <2KB), scroll-triggered reveals with staggered children (content entrance), and background parallax at 0.95x scroll rate (depth). All gated behind prefers-reduced-motion and @media(hover:hover). Custom easing: cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1) — fast snap, gentle float.

Callouts — Margin notes, info, warnings

Three callout styles for different purposes. Yellow for insights and margin notes, blue for informational context, red for warnings and emphasis. Left border accent + tinted background.

Margin note
This is the kind of insight you scribble in the margin of a book. Important enough to flag, not important enough for its own section. Yellow highlight energy — "pay attention to this."
Context
Informational callout for background context, definitions, or supplementary material. Like the "note" boxes in technical documentation but without the clinical feel.
Important
Red callout for warnings, breaking changes, or critical information. Not alarmist — just firm. "You need to know this before proceeding."
