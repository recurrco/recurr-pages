Steel & Manufacturing

इस्पात — that which holds the weight of a country.

India is the world's second-largest steelmaker. 168.4 million tonnes a year, 220 million tonnes of capacity, a target of 300 by 2030. Behind every number is a furnace, a township, a railway rake, a coking-coal cargo. This is the sector that decides what India can build.

The Brief

A country is what it can cast and roll.

Read time
~ 90 seconds

Updated
April 26, 2026

Steel is the most political industrial product India makes. 168.4 million tonnes of crude steel last year, up 10.7%, second only to a slowing China. It builds the railways, the bridges, the cars, the cooking gas cylinders, the rebar in every flyover. It also pays the wages of more than half a million people and the school fees of millions more.

The sector is at an inflection point that is not visible at the surface. Domestic demand is the strongest in the world. Capacity is racing toward 300 million tonnes by 2030. But three structural pressures — Chinese imports, ninety-percent-imported coking coal, and the EU's CBAM carbon levy from January 2026 — are forcing every blast furnace in the country to think about how it operates in 2040.

Vayam covers Steel & Manufacturing because every other sector we cover — defence, transport, infrastructure, energy — is downstream of it. There is no industrial India without steel. And there is no story of steel without the people who make it.

Numbers that matter

Six readings of where the sector stands.

168.4 MT
Crude Steel · FY 2025–26
Up 10.7% YoY. India is the world's second-largest producer behind China (960.8 MT, –4.4%).
300 MTPA
Capacity Target · 2030
From ~220 MTPA today, under the National Steel Policy 2017. Per-capita consumption goal: 158 kg, from ~100 today.
90 %
Coking Coal · Imported
Mostly from Australia, the US, Canada. Mission Coking Coal aims to bring this below 80% by 2030.
15.2 Lakh
Workers · Sector & Adjacent
~5–6 lakh direct in organised steel; another ~25–30 lakh in mining, transport, fabrication, construction. Contract share rising fast.
12 %
Safeguard Duty · Active
On flat steel imports, April 2025 – April 2026. Tapers to 11.5% then 11%. Imports fell 34% YoY in seven months.
€269/t
CBAM · Default HRC
Indicative carbon charge on Indian blast-furnace HRC entering the EU. CBAM definitive regime begins Jan 1, 2026.
The Institutions

The companies, ministries, and bodies that shape this sector.

Public Sector
Maharatna and Navratna producers, miners, and engineering arms.
  • SAIL
    Steel Authority of India · Maharatna · 5 integrated plants + Salem Special Steel · 9M FY26 revenue ₹79,997 Cr
  • RINL · Vizag Steel
    7.3 MTPA shore-based plant · ₹11,440 Cr Cabinet revival package, Jan 2025 · MNVS Prabhakar incoming CMD
  • NMDC Steel
    3 MTPA at Nagarnar, Chhattisgarh · demerged from NMDC in 2022
  • NMDC
    India's largest iron ore producer (~45–50 MT/year) · Bailadila + Donimalai mines
  • MOIL
    India's largest manganese ore producer · 11 mines · target 3.5 MMT by 2030
  • KIOCL · MECON
    Pellet production (Mangalore) and engineering consultancy for steel projects
Private Sector
The four families and one joint venture that define modern Indian steel.
  • Tata Steel
    26.1 MT India crude capacity · founded 1907 as TISCO · CEO T. V. Narendran · Jamshedpur + Kalinganagar + Meramandali
  • JSW Steel
    29.7 MTPA, target 50 by FY31 · Vijayanagar 19 MT (largest single-location in India) · Sajjan Jindal
  • Jindal Steel
    15.6 MTPA, Angul 12 + Raigarh 3.6 · target 25.2 MT by 2030 · Naveen Jindal · ₹70,000 Cr Odisha capex announced
  • AM/NS India
    ArcelorMittal + Nippon Steel JV · acquired Essar Steel Dec 2019 (₹42,000 Cr) · Hazira 12 → 24 MTPA
  • Vedanta–Sesa–ESL
    1.5 MTPA at Bokaro · iron ore mining synergies · Anil Agarwal
  • Kalyani · BHEL · APL Apollo
    Specialty forging steel (defence/auto), heavy engineering capital goods, structural tubes
Ministries & Regulators
Where policy, standards, and trade remedies are set.
  • Ministry of Steel
    Cabinet Minister: H. D. Kumaraswamy · administers PSU steelmakers and sectoral policy
  • Joint Plant Committee (JPC)
    Founded 1964 · primary data agency for the iron and steel industry · custodian, Steel Development Fund
  • Bureau of Indian Standards
    Mandatory ISI mark via ~140 Quality Control Orders for steel products · QCO exemption window expires March 31, 2026
  • DGTR
    Directorate General of Trade Remedies · investigates anti-dumping, safeguard, countervailing duty cases
  • DGMS · Ministry of Coal · Ministry of Mines
    Mine safety; coking coal policy (Mission Coking Coal, 2022); iron ore auctions and the National Mineral Policy
  • PESB · NMET
    CMD selection for steel PSUs; mineral exploration funding

The 90% Problem.
India's coking-coal addiction
and what it costs to break.

India is the world's second-largest steelmaker on the back of coal it does not have. Ninety percent of metallurgical coke is imported. As the country races to 300 million tonnes of capacity by 2030, that addiction is set to deepen — coking coal demand could rise 42% by the end of the decade. This is the longest single piece Vayam will publish at launch — a 6,000-word tour through Bailadila, Bhilai, Jamshedpur, Newcastle, and the policy rooms in Shastri Bhawan that have been trying, and mostly failing, to solve this for forty years.
