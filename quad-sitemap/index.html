Information Architecture · v1

The site, organised around what a buyer actually decides.

Program-first architecture. The conversion spine (what Quad sells), the SEO capture lane (what buyers search), and the trust surfaces (what de-risks the decision) are kept as separate jobs — so nothing competes with itself.

2 schools 4 programs 3 campuses 7 program×campus leaves Open content · one /apply path Live entities only — nothing "coming soon"
Discovery

Search is geo-skewed — "btech colleges in patna", "engineering colleges in mangalore for cs" ($2.80 CPC), "best private university in bangalore". Served by campus leaves + guides.

Conversion

98.6% top-of-funnel leak. 51,519 leads → 706 started → 0 enrolled. Real apps come from Ads/Vedantu/Direct (79% of apps, 1.8% of leads). Open content, one apply path.

Credibility

Two-person buyer: student desire + parent veto (70% kill rate). Parent's question — "is this a real college?" Answered by /degree + a CMS-driven FAQ.

Locked Decisions

the reasoning behind the shape
DecisionChoice & why
Primary axisProgram-first. The program is what a buyer decides on — cleanest page to rank + convert. School = nav grouping, Campus = facet.
Campus pagesHub + indexable campus leaves. Captures high-CPC geo+degree terms a dropdown can't, without fragmenting the flagship.
No /compareCompetitor comparison dropped — too aggressive for the brand now. Comparison intent served quietly inside /guides.
Nothing "coming soon"Design school, B.Des, Mumbai removed. Push them when they're real.
QATDedicated /qat top-level surface — the exam itself as a soft-entry funnel into /apply.
FAQCMS play. Searchable hub + JSON-LD AEO surface + contextual blocks on every page. The answer surface competitors all neglect.
ConversionOpen content, zero gating, one /apply. Attacks the 98.6% leak + the over-gating the current site is blamed for.
HomepagePositioning + dual router. Routes student (program) and parent (campus) fast, without forcing one.
