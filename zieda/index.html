A note from Recurr Studio · 11 April 2026

Migration to a shape.

Your diagnosis was right. It was not complete. This is what we found when we listened, and what we think you should build next.

Opening

An old line of yours, first.

Beauty today is presence. It is the courage to honour your individuality in a world where everyone is trying to be the same. Filters can smooth the skin, but beauty always comes from alignment with self. Zieda Sharipova · Composure Magazine · August 2025

We read every interview we could find. You have written better copy about your own company than any agency you have ever hired. Everything that follows is an argument for a marketing program that finally sounds like you do in these interviews — and an attempt to explain, gently, why it does not yet.

01 — What you told us

The diagnosis you handed us.

On the calls earlier this month, three observations came up in almost identical language. We are writing them back to you because the words matter.

  • Our data is in silos.
  • We have no automation.
  • There are too many moving parts. No one clear owner. No one clear system.

The silos are literal. Four systems with no conversations between them: Zoho for finance and inventory, Zenoti for salon operations, Instapracto for clinic patient records, and Freshworks for every inbound WhatsApp and Instagram lead. Every report produced by hand. Every lead chased manually. Every CRM holding a slightly different version of the truth.

We also watched it happen in real time. During the audit call on 3 April, your dental WhatsApp number did not ring. The salon booking page failed to load. A test lead we submitted through the dental website landed in Freshworks but never made it to Zenoti. None of these were dramatic failures. They were the small, steady kind that quietly lose you bookings every night, particularly after hours.

All of this is true. It is not complete.

02 — What we think is actually broken

A different diagnosis.

When you describe the problem, you describe systems and tools. When we listen, we hear something underneath.

You do not feel in control of your marketing.

Not because your tools are bad. Not because your team is not trying. Because there is no seat in your company today from which the marketing program is fully visible — and because the program itself has no shape yet.

It has parts. Lots of them. Good parts: talented people, real ad spend, real customers, real revenue, a founder who has been on the cover of Emirates Woman and who gives better copy to Composure than most brand teams produce in a year. But the parts do not add up to a shape. A thing without a shape is a thing without leverage. You cannot pull a lever you cannot see.

Tools are a downstream problem. Shape is the upstream one. Fix the shape, and the tools begin to make sense. Fix the tools without the shape, and you will be having the same conversation a year from now, with different software.

03 — 1,500 days from today

Where we want you to be.

Before we describe the shape, we want to tell you where we found it. We found it by refusing to start from today. We started from a date roughly four years out — 1,500 days from this note — and asked a single question: where do you want Zieda to be when it arrives?

Here is the answer we think you already carry in your head. We have reduced it to three headlines.

April 2030 · Feature

Zieda’s opens first store in Singapore.

The Dubai-born beauty group becomes the first GCC aesthetic brand to enter Southeast Asia. A four-floor flagship combining salon, clinic, and Zieda’s own cosmetics line.

April 2029 · Profile

Zieda’s launches own cosmetics.

A premium range built on a decade of clinical experience. Professionals first; consumer launch to follow — a distribution model the group describes as “earned before it is sold.”

April 2027 · Launch

Zieda’s opens beauty academy.

Training and consulting for the GCC’s beauty industry, led by founder Zieda Sharipova. Practitioners, salon owners, and distributors from Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, and the UAE.

You are here: 11 April 2026.

None of the three headlines above can be written without the step we took today, and the step we think you should take next.

04 — The shape

Three layers, one pyramid.

The shape we keep coming back to is a pyramid with three layers. We drew it this way because it is the simplest form we could find that holds the whole marketing program in a single image. Everything that follows in this note, and everything we would propose to build, sits somewhere inside it.

ZIEDA
One brand. One face. One story. One web property. One source of truth for every customer.
Lounge · Clinic
Two funnels. Not two brands. Segments of one audience with different paid budgets and different creative.
Search · Press · Stories
The air people breathe. The owned organic layer that makes the two above it cheaper every month.

The top — one brand.

Today there are three websites, three logo treatments, three colour systems, three booking flows, four CRMs, and two Instagram accounts under one founder’s name. Our proposal is straightforward: one brand, one web property, one customer data platform, one Instagram account that looks like the editorial voice you already have in Vogue Arabia, Harper’s Bazaar, Composure, and L’Officiel. Zenoti and Instapracto move downstream of a single marketing layer that owns every conversation from first touch to booking.

The middle — two funnels, not two brands.

Inside one brand sit two distinct customer journeys: the lounge (upkeep) and the clinic (permanent change). They are not different brands. They are the same customer at different stages of the same story — I want to feel more confident about how I look. Each gets its own paid budget and its own creative, but they share data, share retargeting audiences, and share a single bottom-of-funnel booking experience. A lounge customer today is a clinic customer in two years. A clinic patient can extend her lifetime value with four lounge visits a year. The CRM you build should make that obvious.

The base — the air people breathe.

Below the two funnels sits the largest, quietest, and most undervalued layer: the mass-awareness air that builds category authority over years and makes everything above it cheaper. Search, press, educational content, user-generated reviews, domain authority. Most of the people who consume it will never be your customers. That is exactly why it works. A premium brand is luxurious precisely because a great many people who cannot afford it still know what it stands for. We think this layer is the cheapest competitive advantage currently sitting on the table in Dubai. The next section explains why.

05 — The math

The numbers we worked out.

We spent a week on the math. Three findings shape the rest of this note.

You are sitting on a market you are not in.

The UAE beauty and aesthetic search universe, scoped to commercially intentful keywords — cosmetic dermatology, body contouring, IV therapy, cosmetic dentistry, salon services, and founder-lookalike queries — is approximately 97,000 queries per month. Your three domains together earn approximately 2,521 monthly organic visits from it. That is not a rounding error. It is the single largest finding in this audit.

More striking: of the 80 commercial keywords we scored for difficulty, 64 percent sit at a keyword difficulty under 20, and not a single one crosses KD 40. For reference, “dentist London” is KD 55–75; most B2B SaaS categories live between 60 and 85. This is the lowest-difficulty premium category we have ever audited. Your competitors are winning on volume of service pages and Google Business Profile reviews, not on backlink moats. Which means the market is catchable.

You are invisible at the moment customers decide.

We then pulled every Reddit thread from the last twelve months in which buyers in Dubai asked “where should I go for X?” across r/dubai, r/UAE, and the relevant skincare and dentistry subreddits. Every premium operator in your category gets named at least once: Lucia, Seline, Athena, London Skin, Al Biraa, Avoria, Svorcina, Novomed, Skin Laundry, Minals, Euromed. You were mentioned zero times. In a decision journey where friends and Reddit drive roughly sixty-five percent of premium purchases, you are absent at the most decisive step in the funnel.

The one raw quote that stayed with us, from a new mother on r/dubai in September 2025:

I caught a glimpse of myself and I look really haggard. I feel dull. I used to glow before but I no longer do. Of course beauty isn’t everything but for me it feels like a sense of loss — I do feel I’ve lost myself, so trying to get one part of me back would help. r/dubai · September 2025

That woman is your ideal customer. A month earlier, you told Composure that beauty was about amplifying what already exists within you. The two quotes were written five weeks apart and they answer each other. They have never met, because there is no shape between them.

Lucia has the celebrity. You can have the warmth.

Lucia Clinic — a single-villa, founder-led operation much like yours — earns 14,700 monthly visits to your 2,521, largely on the back of Dr. Radmila Lukian’s 282,000-follower personal Instagram, locked celebrity press in CNN and People, and a trademarked method name (“RL Non-Surgical Face Lift”) that functions as a category of one. That is the model. But read the public reviews and a second picture emerges. One from July 2025:

I had an appointment for Ultherapy — a premium procedure that costs 17,500 AED. I lay there for one hour and twenty minutes in silence. It honestly felt like being invisible. I took a tissue, wiped the gel off my face myself, and walked out. Not one word. Public review, Lucia Clinic · July 2025

Lucia has celebrity and scale. What Lucia does not have, reliably, is warmth. The single most repeated dealbreaker word in the premium Dubai beauty conversation is upselling. The single most repeated reward is she took her time. That is a gap, and it is the gap your brand story was born to fill. You have already said it out loud; you simply have not built the marketing to let anyone else hear it.

06 — What we propose

A shape, not a refresh.

We are not proposing a marketing refresh. We are proposing that you build the first thing you have never had: a shape. Concretely, that means three things, one per layer of the pyramid, over the next twelve months.

  1. Unify the top.

    One Zieda brand. One web property that looks like a magazine and books like a clinic. One customer data platform that sits above Zenoti and Instapracto, not beside them. Every lead, every conversation, every booking lives in one place first — then pushes downstream into the operational systems for fulfilment. Your existing editorial voice (Vogue Arabia, Emirates Woman, L’Officiel, Composure, Harper’s Bazaar) moves into the owned channels it has been locked out of.

  2. Clarify the middle.

    Lounge and clinic become two segments inside one system, not two brands with separate pipes. Each gets its own content pod, its own paid budget line, its own conversion goal. The data and the brand are shared. A lounge visit becomes a clinic lead automatically; a clinic patient is cross-sold a lounge plan on month three.

  3. Take the base.

    Ship forty well-optimised service pages in the first six months, add Russian and Chinese language mirrors (a moat none of your competitors can easily copy), activate the dental domain on the aesthetic-dentistry keyword cluster that currently has near-zero competition, and turn your existing press footprint into compounding organic authority via schema and a real press wall. Base case: 5,850 to 7,800 monthly organic visits by year end. Bull case: over ten thousand. The ceiling is execution speed, not strategy.

This is what we came to tell you.

If any of it is wrong, we would rather hear it now than six months from now. If most of it is right, the work begins with a single decision: agree that the program needs a shape before it needs any more tools. Everything else follows from that.

— Pratik Mishra, on behalf of Recurr Studio.
