03 — FY25-26 Retrospective

What we built.
What we didn't.

Built

  • Infrastructure. Workspace, tools, project management (Outwork), design system, full technical stack.
  • Brand identity. recurr.co live with 7 pages on V5 design system.
  • Editorial architecture. S3 vision defined. 175 articles extracted and ready for processing.

Didn't

  • Consistent revenue. Client work was sporadic. Engagements started and dropped without follow-on.

04A — Agency

Communication design applied to client businesses. Every client gets an account manager, a delivery team, and a performance review cycle. This is Recurr's core DNA — scaling the agency is not a problem.

How it works

04B — Studio Model

The model.

Every Studio platform follows the same playbook:

Traditional service (food ordering, compliance, merchandise) ↓ Wrap it with technology (marketplace, portal, ecommerce) ↓ Layer media on top (content, campaigns, editorial) ↓ Revenue = commission on transactions, not flat fees ↓ Scale with partnerships, not headcount

The agency supports every Studio platform with marketing and pipeline work. Studio builds generate assets (content, case studies, data) that feed back into the agency's portfolio and recurr.co/press. The three arms are not silos — they feed each other.

04C — Vayam

Vayam.

A media vertical covering the intersection of economy, policy, and frontier technology in India. Minerals, mining, defence, energy, critical infrastructure, quantum, AI — the economic layers that power the country but are barely covered.

This is a long-term strategic bet with zero revenue expectations in Year 1. The ambition is to become the most recognisable part of Recurr over the next five years — and to eventually serve as a bridge to governance and policy communication.

Q1 goal: Go non-zero. Slow and small is fine. First publication out. Prove the format works.

Why this exists

  • Frontier sectors have few players, all capital-rich, all bad at marketing.
  • Thought leadership is the primary marketing channel in these sectors.
  • Extremely high ACV. Less competitive. Policy-sensitive.
  • Visual explainers that make difficult concepts accessible to India's consumers.

First project: Anthracite

  • Book: 15 essays across 4 themes.
  • Content-first: each essay is a standalone article. All 15 together form the book.
  • Theme II (The Critical Minerals Pivot) in production.
  • Establishes Vayam's editorial voice and production pipeline.
05 — Delivery

The creative bench.

Recurr's delivery engine is a small, high-agency creative team. The bench supports all three arms — Agency clients, Studio platforms, and Vayam editorial. Delivery is led directly by the founder.

The bench manages the work — scheduling, coordination, execution. Creative direction and quality control come from the top. The team doesn't replace the founder's craft. It amplifies it.

We scale by adding capacity to the bench, not by adding management layers. The creative bench is a feature, not a bottleneck.

09 — Risks

What could go wrong.

Spreading too thin

Three arms, five Studio platforms, multiple Agency clients. Small team.

Sequence ruthlessly. Canteen is P0. JH10 is build-only. BLR/Waterpost/MerchMag are Q2. Don't start everything at once.

Press doesn't publish

The content engine never turns on. Agency has no inbound pipeline.

175 articles are the cold start. 2/week minimum. AI-assisted production. This is non-negotiable.

Studio platforms don't find PMF

Canteen, JH10, or others fail to get traction.

Commission model means low fixed cost. Fail fast, learn, pivot. Agency revenue covers the base.

Agency stays network-dependent

Inbound doesn't materialise. Leads come from founder's network only.

Press + recurr.co must be live and publishing by April. LinkedIn activation. Build the machine, don't rely on relationships.

Key person dependency

Founder is creative director, chief editor, Studio lead, and MOO strategist.

The creative bench amplifies, it doesn't replace. Accept this as a feature in Year 1. Build capacity for Year 2.

10 — Principles

How we operate.

Every rupee has a thesis

No spend without a clear expected outcome. Always know what success looks like before committing capital.

Practice what we preach

recurr.co is Client Zero. Our own content engine must work before we sell it to others.

Service wrapped in tech

Studio platforms are not software products. They are traditional services redesigned with technology and media as the delivery layer.

Sequence, don't parallelise

Ship one thing well before starting the next. Canteen first. Then JH10. Then BLR. Discipline over ambition.
