The Shift

Software was the science of the previous era. You bought a license, onboarded your team, climbed the learning curve — all to get a few jobs done. Each tool was rigid, each workflow was manual. Like the steam engine. Functional, but slow.

Then came LLMs. And they made software feel stupid. LLMs are flexible, powerful — they learn and operate on your behalf. You don’t sit inside the machine and operate it. You interact with it and it operates on your behalf. You can do better things. More things.

This shift is too big to think of as an incremental, linear change. Intelligence is a whole new dimension. Our possibilities just went from 2D to 3D. We just realised the world is not flat.

The math changed
LLM + your team member can address exponentially more jobs to be done than software + your team member ever could. Anthropic’s marketing for its first 10 months was run by a single marketer. That’s the scale of change we’re talking about.
But Right Now

Your teams don’t know how to use it. Everyone is still unlearning and learning — there’s too much happening every day to catch up. Most “AI” software is actually stuck in the software era. And so you keep hoarding bookmarks, using LLMs like you always used software. All the potential. No method to harness it. Every passing day.

These problems will get solved — better tools will come, teams will adapt. But till then, your growth functions are running on 2015-era methodology with a ChatGPT tab open on the side.
