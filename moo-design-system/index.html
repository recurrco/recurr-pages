Principles

What MOO looks like

Comic book energy

Bold type, bright pops, starburst moments. Everything feels like it's about to leap off the page. Not subtle. Not trying to be.

Caricature-driven

6 bunny characters own the categories. They're brand IP. They appear on packaging, social, kiosks, the website. Characters make MOO recognizable in a way no cloud kitchen has ever been.

Dark by default

F&B on dark backgrounds. Lets food photography pop. The grid pattern on blue/dark surfaces is our texture — like graph paper, but confident.

Multi-surface

Three surface modes: cream (editorial, reading), dark (hero, impact), blue + grid (brand moments, social). Each serves a different emotional register.

Palette

Four colors. No negotiation.

Blue is home. Yellow is energy. Red is action. Cream is comfort. Black holds them all together.

Surfaces

Three modes. Three moods.

Every page section lives on one of three surfaces. The grid pattern is the MOO texture — it appears on dark and blue surfaces only.

Cream

Editorial, reading, recipes. Warm and inviting. Clean food energy.

Dark

Hero sections, impact moments. Grid overlay at 4% opacity.

Blue + Grid

Brand moments, social, CTAs. Grid overlay at 8% opacity. The signature MOO look.

Typography

Bold, heavy, unapologetic.

Display font does the heavy lifting. Body stays clean. Mono handles metadata. Everything is system fonts — no web font loading.

Texture

The Grid.

Thin lines, 40px spacing. White at 8% on blue, 4% on dark. Like graph paper from a design lab. It's on every MOO social post, every blue background, every dark hero. It's ours.

CSS:
background-image:
  linear-gradient(rgba(255,255,255,0.08) 1px, transparent 1px),
  linear-gradient(90deg, rgba(255,255,255,0.08) 1px, transparent 1px);
background-size: 40px 40px;

Rules

The non-negotiables.

1. Blue (#4747ED) is the primary brand color. It's home. Every page has blue somewhere.
2. Yellow for energy (DIY, starbursts, labels on dark). Red for action (CTAs, alerts, "Feed Me Already").
3. Cream for editorial. Dark for hero. Blue+grid for brand moments.
4. Grid pattern on blue and dark surfaces. NEVER on cream.
5. Caricatures own the categories. One character per category. They don't mix.
6. Display font weight 800-900 for all headlines. Never light. Never thin.
7. Interactions bounce (ease-bounce). 120ms. Everything feels alive.
8. Food photography > illustrations > text. The food is the hero.
9. Every piece of copy passes the "Never Be Boring" filter. If it's boring, rewrite it.
10. Border-radius: pill (100px) for buttons/badges. Medium (12px) for cards. Small (6px) for callouts.
