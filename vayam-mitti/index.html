A palette from the ground up.

India's industrial identity was written first in its soil. Before the mills and the mines, before the mobile towers and the solar fields, there was the ground — basalt and alluvium, laterite and loam — each grain deciding what a region would grow, what it would build, what it would become. This is Vayam's palette. Eight soils. Eight stories. One country.

Eight soil families · ICAR classification Version 0.1 · draft for review

Most brand palettes begin with a mood board. Ours begins with a core sample. India is not one landscape — it is a mosaic of soils, each the product of a different geological accident: a volcano in the Deccan, a glacier in the Himalaya, a monsoon in Kerala, a dry wind in Thar.

Each soil became an industry. Cotton on the black Regur. Steel from the red iron ores. Aluminium from laterite. Salt from the saline crust of Kutch. Tea from mountain humus. The soil is not a backdrop to India's industry — it is the industry, in its earliest, most honest form.

Vayam covers the frontier sectors of India. So our palette starts at the frontier of any sector — the ground beneath it.

जलोढ़
Alluvial #C9AE7D

Alluvial / जलोढ़

जलोढ़ मिट्टी — the soil that arrived by water.
Indo-Gangetic Plain · Brahmaputra Valley · 40% of India's land
Origin
Two thousand kilometres of Himalayan debris, carried by the Indus, the Ganga, and the Brahmaputra, laid down grain by grain across the northern plain. The softest, flattest, most forgiving surface on the subcontinent.
Harvest
Wheat. Rice. Sugarcane. Jute. The calorie base for 800 million people.
Industry
The Green Revolution. Sugar mills from Meerut to Motihari. The food-processing belt. The irrigation economy. Every granary from Punjab to Bengal sits on alluvium.
Frontier
Agri-tech, cold chain, biofuel, climate-smart irrigation. The soil that fed India is the soil that will test whether India can feed itself sustainably.
02 / 08
रेगुर
Black · Regur #2B2922

Black Soil / रेगुर

काली मिट्टी — the self-ploughing earth.
Deccan Trap · Maharashtra · Madhya Pradesh · Gujarat · Northern Karnataka
Origin
Sixty-six million years ago, a volcanic province the size of France erupted in central India. The basalt cooled. The basalt broke down. What remained is the deepest, darkest, most moisture-hungry soil in the country — cracks so wide in summer they swallow a goat, swells so tight in monsoon they plough themselves.
Harvest
Cotton. India's white gold. Also sorghum, pulses, oilseeds.
Industry
The cotton mills of Mumbai and Ahmedabad. Every textile exporter India has ever had. The railway lines that carried cotton to Bombay to Manchester and back. An entire colonial economy, and then a post-colonial one, built on a soil that ploughs itself.
Frontier
Technical textiles, regenerative cotton, circular apparel, bio-based dyes. If India writes the playbook for sustainable textiles, it will be written on Regur.
03 / 08
लाल
Red Soil #A94A3C

Red Soil / लाल मिट्टी

लाल मिट्टी — the rusted ground.
Tamil Nadu · Karnataka · Odisha · Jharkhand · Eastern Madhya Pradesh
Origin
Archaean granite, weathered across two and a half billion years. The iron inside the rock oxidised, rusted, and stained the ground red. Less fertile than alluvium. More patient than black.
Harvest
Millets. Groundnut. Pulses. Cotton where black soil runs thin. The crops of the drylands.
Industry
What the soil couldn't grow, it held beneath. India's iron-ore belt runs through the same red earth — Singhbhum, Bailadila, Bellary. Tata Steel's Jamshedpur. SAIL. The entire steel economy, built on the iron that dyed the ground.
Frontier
Green steel, hydrogen-based DRI, critical minerals, battery-grade ores. The red belt holds the raw materials of the next industrial revolution, not just the last one.
04 / 08
लैटराइट
Laterite #8B3A2A

Laterite / लैटराइट

लैटराइट — literally "the brick soil".
Western Ghats · Eastern Ghats · Kerala · Konkan · Malabar · Chhota Nagpur
Origin
Monsoon-leached. Tropical rain so heavy it washes out the silica and leaves only iron and aluminium oxides behind. You can cut it into blocks straight out of the ground, and it hardens in the sun. Half of Kerala's old houses are made of it.
Harvest
Cashew. Tapioca. Rubber. Coffee on the higher slopes. Spice gardens in the folds.
Industry
Bauxite. India's aluminium sector — NALCO, Hindalco, Vedanta — sits on laterite and its parent rock. The metal that becomes foil, cans, wire, aircraft, EV chassis.
Frontier
Low-carbon aluminium, recycled-content alloys, bauxite-to-battery value chains. The material India will export as the world electrifies.
05 / 08
मरु
Arid · Desert #D9B47A

Arid Soil / मरुस्थलीय

मरुस्थलीय मिट्टी — the soil of a dry wind.
Thar Desert · Western Rajasthan · Kutch · Parts of Haryana and Punjab
Origin
Sand blown east by millennia of wind, mixed with calcium carbonate nodules — the kankar that makes wells and foundations possible. Low on organic matter, high on minerals, thirsty by nature.
Harvest
Bajra. Guar. Dates. The dryland crops that ask nothing and return enough.
Industry
Limestone and cement (J.K., Shree, Ambuja). Marble out of Makrana. Salt and soda ash. And now the world's largest single-site solar park at Bhadla — 2,245 MW of panels on a soil that grew nothing else.
Frontier
Gigawatt-scale solar, green hydrogen, grid-scale storage, low-carbon cement. The desert is India's energy frontier. The soil nobody wanted is the soil powering everything else.
06 / 08
ऊसर
Saline · Kallar #D5CFC2

Saline Soil / ऊसर

ऊसर / कल्लर — the earth that shines white.
Rann of Kutch · Coastal Gujarat · Parts of Punjab, Haryana, UP
Origin
Groundwater rising through evaporation, leaving sodium, calcium and magnesium salts crusted white on the surface. A soil that has been too well watered for too long, in the wrong chemistry. Infertile for most crops. Perfect for one thing.
Harvest
Salt. Seventy-five percent of India's salt comes from the Little Rann and the Gulf of Kutch.
Industry
The chemicals corridor. Soda ash for glass. Caustic soda for soap. Bromine. The Agariya salt-pan workers, the Tata Chemicals plant at Mithapur, the entire industrial-chemistry map of western Gujarat — on a soil that refused to grow food.
Frontier
Green chemicals, lithium-from-brine, desalination, bromine-based energy storage. The barren ground turning into a battery.
07 / 08
दलदली
Peaty · Marshy #231912

Peaty Soil / दलदली

दलदली मिट्टी — the waterlogged earth.
Kerala Backwaters · Sundarbans · Coastal Odisha · Pockets of Tamil Nadu
Origin
Thousands of years of plant matter, drowned and preserved in waterlogged ground. The darkest, richest, wettest soil in India — in places you can set it on fire once it dries.
Harvest
Rice in Kuttanad, the only farmland in India that sits below sea level. Coconut. Coir. Jute along the delta.
Industry
Coir and coco-peat — the original bio-material export. Aquaculture and shrimp. Mangroves, which the world now counts as one of the densest carbon stores on earth.
Frontier
Blue carbon markets, bio-composites, climate-resilient aquaculture, coastal adaptation. The soil that absorbs floods may become the soil that funds them off.
08 / 08
पर्वत
Forest · Mountain #4A3728

Forest Soil / पर्वतीय

पर्वतीय मिट्टी — the humus of the high country.
Himalayas · Western Ghats · Eastern Ghats · Nilgiris · Northeast
Origin
Leaf fall and root rot, a thousand autumns deep. Loamy, acidic, rich in organic matter. On the steep slopes it is thin and brittle; in the valleys it is deep and dark.
Harvest
Tea. Coffee. Cardamom. Apples. The forest economy of India's ridgelines.
Industry
Assam and Darjeeling plantations. Nilgiri tea. Coorg coffee. Himalayan hydropower — the rivers that descend through forest soil power the cities in the plains below. Timber and paper when the forests are let go; carbon and tourism when they are kept.
Frontier
Pumped hydro, run-of-river, traceable specialty produce, climate-resilient plantations, forest carbon. The soil that measures how well India balances growth and ground.

How the palette works.

The eight soils are not equal-weight. Most work happens on Paper, in Ink. Soils arrive as identifiers — a section, a sector, a story. Red is the only colour that ever plays a pure accent role.

  • Primary surfacePaper (#F6F2EA) for all editorial. Ink (#141210) for hero surfaces and long reads.
  • Sector codingOne soil per frontier sector. Textile stories live on Regur. Steel on Red. Solar on Desert. Aluminium on Laterite.
  • AccentRed Soil (#A94A3C) for CTAs, editor's notes, and the drop-cap. Never decorative.
  • NeverNo pure white. No pure black. No gradients between soils. No soil used as a background for body copy longer than a pullquote.
  • ScriptDevanagari is reserved: it appears as a watermark in swatches and as a signature in the footer. Never for running text.
