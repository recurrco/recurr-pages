The idea

Two layers. One identity.

Crimson and gold are the enduring face of Quad — bold, grounded, serious. On top sits a contemporary layer of colour and pattern that keeps the brand ambitious and alive. The first reassures the parent; the second inspires the student. Together they read as an institution with a legacy, not a startup with a logo.

The enduring layer

Bold & prestigious

Crimson on ivory, one disciplined typeface, all-caps eyebrows under a thin rule, gold used for emphasis. Generous space and honest, documentary photography. This never changes — it is what makes Quad feel permanent.

The contemporary layer

Spectrum & pattern

A refined colour spectrum — one hue per school, all kin to the house crimson — and four geometric patterns drawn directly from the Quad mark. Bold, full, and used with intent. This is what makes Quad feel built for what's next.

Pattern construction

One grid. Three densities. No guesswork.

Every pattern is built on an 8px grid so it tiles cleanly at any scale. Pick a density by surface size — and the stroke scales with it so the optical weight stays constant. When in doubt, use Default.

Photography & social

Real students. Real campus. One treatment.

Quad's strength is honest, documentary photography — actual students mid-work, presenting, building. The system gives every shot one repeatable treatment: a light wash in the school accent, a soft pattern layered over it, and the same headline frame. (Hover a tile to see the pattern drift.)

Actual imagery from quadcse.com — candid, warm, real faces. This is the raw material; never replace it with stock.

The social treatment

Do

  • Keep the wash light — the student stays the hero
  • Layer one soft pattern motif over the photo, in gold
  • Shoot real students, real moments — candid over posed
  • Crop tight on faces, hands, and the work itself
  • Lock the same eyebrow-bar + headline frame every time

Don't

  • Crank the overlay so heavy the face disappears
  • Use generic stock or AI-generated "students"
  • Stack two patterns or let the motif fight the face
  • Crowd the frame — protect the scrim and breathing room
  • Set headlines in a different font or all-caps blocks
