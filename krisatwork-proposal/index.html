Emerging territory — where competitors haven't moved yet

The established categories — "AI sales assistant," "meeting transcription," "sales coaching" — are crowded. What's forming now, and still largely unclaimed, is a different set of conversations. These are the ones we go after first.

Sales rep time reclamation

Sales leaders are asking: "Why are my reps spending most of their time on admin and not selling?" This is a management and operations search — not a product search. Competitors are speaking to individual reps. Nobody is speaking to the VP or RevOps leader trying to solve this at a team level. This is open territory.

↳ No dominant content player here yet
Tool consolidation — the SaaS fatigue narrative

Teams are drowning in point solutions. There is a growing search behaviour around "how to reduce the number of tools my sales team uses" and "unified AI for sales teams." Gong, Sybill, and Read.ai are all point solutions — they add to the problem. Krisatwork, as a unified companion, is positioned to own this narrative. No one is writing for it yet.

↳ Krisatwork's positioning advantage — unclaimed in search and AI answers
Pre-meeting prep intelligence

The post-call space (transcription, summaries, coaching) is saturated. Pre-call — how to walk into a meeting prepared, with the right context, and ready to have a real conversation — is almost unaddressed in content. The buyer behaviour is forming: sales professionals are starting to ask AI how to prepare for specific calls. Krisatwork can own this frame before competitors notice it.

↳ Competitor focus is post-call. Pre-call is open.
AI-first sales workflow design

A newer search behaviour: revenue leaders asking how to redesign their team's workflows around AI — not bolt it on. "How to build an AI-native sales process" has almost no dedicated content from product companies. The content that exists is generic and ChatGPT-written. Krisatwork has the product depth to own this conversation with authority.

↳ Strategic-level search, decision-maker audience, early-stage content landscape

The Plan

3 months. Each with a clear output.

Month 1 fixes what's broken and maps the terrain. Month 2 builds content. Month 3 earns authority and expands AI presence.

01 Month 1 — Fix & Map Foundation
  • ✓Fix the technical blocker — The site currently can't be properly indexed by Google. We fix this in week one. Everything else builds on this.
  • ✓Set up Google Search Console — Submit the sitemap, verify indexing, establish the baseline. This becomes our reporting dashboard for the program.
  • ✓Full keyword and prompt map — Final list of all target search topics and AI prompts, prioritised by effort and opportunity. This is the content roadmap for months 2 and 3.
  • ✓G2 and Capterra profiles — Create verified listings. AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini) pull heavily from G2 when recommending products. No profile means no citation.
  • ✓Baseline audit — Run all 15 target prompts manually across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity. Record what appears and what doesn't. This is the Week 1 starting point we measure against.
  • ✓Content briefs × 4 articles — Detailed briefs for the first four pieces. Each brief maps the search topic, the AI prompt it answers, and the exact structure the content needs to follow.
02 Month 2 — Build & Place Content
  • ✓Publish 4 articles — Targeting problem-aware searches and problem prompts (B1–B5). Written to rank in Google and to be cited in AI answers. These are not generic blog posts.
  • ✓Optimise existing pages — Title tags, headings, meta descriptions on all live pages. Small changes with disproportionate impact once the site is indexed.
  • ✓Roundup outreach — first wave — Reach out to 3 high-authority "best AI sales tool" articles (e.g. Zapier, Lindy) to have Krisatwork added. These are the pages AI tools cite most. Being listed here directly improves AI citation presence. Note: placement on high-authority third-party sites may carry an additional cost depending on the domain.
  • ✓Product Hunt launch — Prepare and launch. AI tools crawl Product Hunt for new product entries. A strong launch day creates a citation-worthy page.
  • ✓First AI prompt check — Rerun the 15 prompts. Compare to baseline. Report what moved and what didn't. Adjust the plan if needed.
03 Month 3 — Earn Authority Compounding
  • ✓2 more articles — Category and comparison content. "Krisatwork vs Gong" comparison. One piece on tool consolidation for revenue teams. These target mid-funnel and decision-stage searches.
  • ✓G2 reviews campaign — Work with the Krisatwork customer team to collect 10+ verified reviews. G2 profiles with 10 or more reviews are cited significantly more often in AI answers than empty ones.
  • ✓Roundup outreach — second wave — 3 additional targets: sales-focused blogs and newsletters with strong AI citation footprint (Sales Hacker, RevGenius, nooks.ai). Note: placement cost may apply on select sites.
  • ✓HubSpot / Salesforce marketplace listing — If not already live. Marketplace pages generate citations and trust signals in AI answers. Competitors use these actively.
  • ✓Full 90-day report — All 15 prompts re-audited. Keyword rankings reviewed. Comparison against Week 1 baseline. What improved, what needs more time, and what we do next.
  • ✓Month 4–6 brief — Based on what worked, we prepare the roadmap for the next cycle before this one ends.
