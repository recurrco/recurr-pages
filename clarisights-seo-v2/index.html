Philip's Warning: Creative Analytics Positioning Constraint

"We are not setting ourselves up for a great first impression if we overly emphasize creative analytics." — Philip (Clarisights team)

The product gaps that competitors fill include:

  • Motion — visual creative reports, AI auto-tagging, benchmarks
  • Alison.ai — element-level analysis, competitive intelligence, AI assistant
  • Smartly.io — creative production + optimization loop

Workflow gaps likely include: automated creative tagging, predictive creative scoring, creative-to-insight recommendations, and competitive creative intelligence. These are features pure creative analytics tools offer that a reporting platform doesn't.

Content strategy implication: Clarisights should NOT try to out-content Motion on creative testing or Improvado on data integration. Instead: own "cross-channel creative performance" (uncontested intersection), own "enterprise creative analytics" (underserved buyer segment), and bridge data integration + creative intelligence in content — a claim only Clarisights can credibly make.

Positioning Constraints

8 hard rules for all Clarisights content, derived from product-content synthesis across 16 product-gap analyses, 10 customer intelligence files, and explicit feedback from Philip, Jan, Avi, Anshul, and Arun.

DO NOT (Rules 1-5)

These constraints protect against overpromising on capabilities the product can't deliver today.

1
DO NOT lead with creative analytics. Philip's warning: "We are not setting ourselves up for a great first impression if we overly emphasize creative analytics." Can't show FB + Google creative performance side by side. Being used as a sales hook but product disappoints. Alison.ai is the specialized competitor (used by Zalando). Use creative analytics as a secondary capability only.
2
DO NOT promise self-serve. Avi's assessment: "Any user working on Clarisights is guaranteed to need support to be able to achieve anything meaningful." Every customer needs hand-holding. Position as "managed analytics" or "supported analytics" if anything. Cursor/Faire loss proves truly self-serve analytics wins some segments.
3
DO NOT promise real-time data. Architecture doesn't support it. Enpal wants intra-day, Welltech wants hourly. Mozek V3 migration needed. "Near real-time" or "daily sync" is honest. Don't use "real-time" in any content.
4
DO NOT position as measurement/attribution. Jan's pushback: "long uphill battle to explain we don't do Measurement." Clear category: cross-channel marketing reporting and analytics. Frame Clarisights as the REPORTING layer that sits ON TOP of attribution data (from AppsFlyer, Adjust, etc.).
5
DO NOT target analytics teams. They'll self-serve with Cursor/Looker. Faire replaced Clarisights with Cursor. Lion Studios went to Segwise's self-serve tool. Target marketing leaders and performance marketing managers — the people who can't code and need governance, not the analysts who will build their own.

DO (Rules 6-8)

These are the pillars every piece of content should reinforce.

6
DO lead with the reporting layer. This is the moat (Anshul's framing). Funnel does ETL. Fivetran does ETL. Looker does BI. GA4 does web analytics. Clarisights does marketing reporting. Nobody else does cross-channel marketing reporting natively for marketing teams. Every content piece should reinforce this category positioning.
7
DO lead with cross-channel aggregation. 15+ channels, one view, custom dimensions. Wolt runs Funnel + Looker + AppsFlyer. Superbet has 5 teams across 3 markets. The pain of multi-tool stacks is the core JTBD. Content should always reference specific channel counts, team sizes, and spend levels.
8
DO use practitioner voice. Philip's SKAN article is the template. Expert-authored, specific, no fluff. Not "Complete Guide to X" generic content. Not marketing-speak. The voice of someone who has actually pulled data from 15 ad platforms and knows why the numbers don't match.

How Competitors See Clarisights

From SegmentStream and Spectacle HQ competitive analyses:

Strength: "Pulls data from 300+ sources, automatically joins and normalizes it down to creative level, and lets marketers build custom dashboards without waiting for the data team."
Weakness: "It is a reporting layer, not a measurement platform." "There are no automated recommendations, no budget optimization, no action triggers — the platform shows you what happened."

This is exactly why the positioning constraints matter. The content strategy must lean into the reporting-layer strength, not fight against it. "Shows you what happened across all your channels" is the value proposition.
