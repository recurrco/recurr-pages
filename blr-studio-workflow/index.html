01

The principle: AI-native, not AI-slop

AI is the leverage — it never replaces the product.
The split

AI does 70–90% of production

Humans own taste, story, and the craft bar — the things 2026 AI still can't do (narrative pacing, emotional beats, brand nuance).

The multiplier

1 source → many assets

One researched, well-made source is atomised into 10–30 shorts, carousels, quote cards, posts. Research once, milk everywhere.

The 5th member

An invisible agent layer

An n8n + agent layer — built & run by Button Builders — does the repetitive orchestration. Humans never touch it.

02

Sanity is the spine

Not a CMS — the workflow nervous system every project lives on.
Page-first

The page is the master asset. YT & IG are cuts of it.

One research-and-write effort produces the designed page; everything else is butchered out of it. Every piece is a document that moves through states — nothing stalls, nothing is left un-milked.

Plan · upstream → Queue→ Research→ Write→ Design / Publish→ PAGE — master
   └─ cut into → YouTubeInstagram · reels · carousels · stories
Sanity configures (Workflow Manager plugin) — it doesn't get built from scratch.
Sanity owns

The queue/kanban · structured content (atoms pull downstream) · workflow state · publish trigger · cross-channel programming checklist.

Sanity holds

The research dossier & visual assets — they just dock to the card.

Sanity stays out of

Doing the research · the visual design (lives in the page template) · the editing/production (real tools).

06

Economics & the two open calls

The headline

< one junior hire

The whole production + growth stack costs ~₹19–23K/mo — only ~7–9% on top of the ₹2.55L salary budget. You spend the price of one salary on software that replaces ~8 of them.

Fully-loaded ≈ ₹2.85–3L/mo (salaries + tools + rent) — the real "cover its own costs" number for the Dec-2026 hard stop.

Two trade-offs — your call

Editing: keep CapCut, or let LTX do it?
One fewer tool via LTX, but editors already know CapCut. Rec: keep CapCut (familiar > minimal here).
Images: decided → Freepik / Magnific
~10 image models (incl. Flux) + stock library + upscaler, one login. Runner-up Krea (64+ models). Midjourney has no API → deferred, standalone only if the craft bar demands.
