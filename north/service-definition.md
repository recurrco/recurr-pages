## Positioning

**One-liner:** We design, build, and operate the product marketing function for B2B software teams.

**Category:** Productised product marketing — intelligence-first, AI-operated.

**Core thesis:** Product marketing is the most knowledge-intensive work in B2B. The job is about consuming massive amounts of information — deals, calls, customer notes, market research, product roadmaps, analyst reports, CRM data, forums, thought leadership — building intelligence from it, structuring it into consumable knowledge, and then shipping that knowledge as collaterals that help every other team do their job better.

This workflow has always been brutally manual. PMMs spend 80% of their time gathering and organising information, and 20% creating the things that actually move the needle.

LLMs changed the equation. The consumption, structuring, and distillation layers are now ideal for AI agents. What used to take weeks of reading, tagging, and synthesis can now be set up as continuous intelligence streams. The PMM's time is freed up to ship higher-value work in higher volume.

North does this at scale. We connect to your systems, build a live intelligence layer, produce the structured middleware your organisation needs, and ship the collaterals that help your teams engage better with their stakeholders.

**What it replaces:** The PMM who spends all their time in spreadsheets and never ships. The agency that doesn't understand your product. The founder doing positioning in a Google Doc at midnight.

**What it is NOT:** A tool. Not a dashboard. Not a SaaS product. North is an operated service — we embed into your systems and run the function.

## The Three Layers

Product marketing work, in every company, follows the same three-layer workflow. The specifics differ — different CRM, different call tool, different conversation platforms — but the structure is universal.

### Layer 1: Intelligence
Connect to the real feeds. Pull live data from the systems your team already uses.

**Sources we tap into:**
- Team conversations (Slack, Zulip, Teams)
- Sales calls and recordings (Gong, Chorus, Fireflies)
- CRM data (HubSpot, Salesforce, Attio)
- Customer notes and tickets (Intercom, Zendesk, Notion)
- Market research and analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester, G2)
- Product roadmaps and changelogs
- Competitor websites, forums, and social
- Thought leadership and industry content

**What happens here:** Raw intelligence is continuously pulled, tagged, and structured. Not a one-time audit — a living feed. This is the gold mine that most companies sit on without realising it.

### Layer 2: Middleware
Turn raw intelligence into structured, usable knowledge artifacts that the entire organisation can draw from.

**What we produce:**
- ICP documentation (who you sell to, why they buy, what they care about)
- Messaging matrix (positioning by persona, use case, competitive frame)
- Competitive intelligence profiles (strengths, weaknesses, positioning, gaps)
- Win/loss analysis (patterns across deals — why you win, why you lose)
- Customer profiles and segments (behavioral, firmographic, use-case based)
- Product intelligence (what's shipping, what matters, how it maps to buyer needs)
- Category context (market landscape, buyer journey, evaluation criteria)

**What happens here:** The middleware is the most valuable layer. It's the company's product marketing brain — structured, current, and accessible. Every collateral, every deck, every page draws from this.

### Layer 3: Collaterals
Ship the things that other teams need to engage better with their stakeholders.

**What we produce:**
- Sales decks and battle cards
- Buyer guides and use case pages
- Marketing pages and messaging
- Feature rundowns and product one-pagers
- Onboarding documents and email journeys
- FAQs and objection handling guides
- Customer engagement templates (for CSM teams)
- Board and investor narratives
- Documentation and knowledge base content

**What happens here:** With the middleware in place, collateral production is fast and consistent. Every piece draws from the same structured intelligence. No more one-off requests that take weeks.

## How It Works

1. **Connect** — We plug into your existing systems. Whatever you use — CRM, call tools, team chat, project management — we set up the intelligence streams.

2. **Build** — We construct the middleware layer. ICP docs, messaging matrix, competitive intel, win/loss patterns. This is the foundation everything else draws from.

3. **Ship** — We produce collaterals on cadence. Sales needs a new battle card? It's built from the middleware. Marketing needs updated positioning? It's already structured. New feature launching? The messaging is ready.

4. **Compound** — The intelligence layer gets richer over time. Every call recorded, every deal closed, every competitor move tracked — it all feeds back in. The longer it runs, the sharper the output.

## The AI Angle

Every company's systems of record are different. Different CRM, different call tool, different team conversation platform. Product marketing looks slightly different at every company because each one sells a different product.

But the workflow is universal. And with current LLM capabilities, it's the most effectively productisable knowledge work in B2B:

- **Consumption** → AI agents read and tag across all connected sources continuously
- **Structuring** → AI agents synthesise patterns, extract themes, flag changes
- **Distillation** → AI agents produce first drafts of middleware artifacts
- **Production** → AI agents generate collateral drafts from structured middleware

The PMM's job shifts from gathering and organising (low-leverage) to reviewing, refining, and making strategic decisions (high-leverage). Same person, 10x output.

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Primary:** B2B software companies with at least one marketing team member.

**Sweet spot:**
- Companies where the product is complex enough that positioning matters
- Teams that have sales calls, CRM data, and customer conversations — but no structured way to turn them into marketing intelligence
- Companies where the founder or a single marketer is doing PMM work manually
- Teams that have tried agencies but found they don't understand the product deeply enough

**Stage:** Series A to Series C. Enough data to be useful, enough complexity to need structure.

**Not for:**
- Pre-product companies (nothing to market yet)
- Companies with a mature 5+ person PMM team (they need tools, not us)
- Companies that think PMM is "writing blog posts"
