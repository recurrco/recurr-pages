The Problem

Every B2B software company sits on a gold mine of intelligence — buried in sales calls, CRM notes, team conversations, customer tickets, competitor moves, and market shifts. The product marketer’s job is to consume all of this, build intelligence from it, and turn it into things every other team can use.

But the work is brutally manual. PMMs spend 80% of their time gathering and organising information, and 20% creating the things that actually move the needle. The systems of record differ at every company — different CRM, different call tool, different team chat — which makes it even harder to systematise.

The shift
With current LLM capabilities, product marketing is the most effectively productisable knowledge work in B2B. The consumption, structuring, and distillation layers are now ideal for AI agents — leaving the product marketer to ship higher-value work in higher volume. Same person, 10x output.
Three Layers

Product marketing work, in every company, follows the same three-layer structure. The specifics differ — but the workflow is universal.

Layer 01
Intelligence
Connect to the real feeds. Pull live data from the systems your team already uses. Not a one-time audit — a living, continuous stream.
Slack / Zulip / Teams Gong / Chorus HubSpot / Salesforce Intercom / Zendesk Gartner / G2 Product roadmaps Competitor sites Forums & social
Layer 02
Middleware
Turn raw intelligence into structured, usable knowledge artifacts. This is the company’s product marketing brain — structured, current, and accessible. Every collateral draws from this.
ICP documentation Messaging matrix Competitive intel Win/loss analysis Customer profiles Product intelligence Category context Buyer journey maps
Layer 03
Collaterals
Ship the things that other teams need to engage better with their stakeholders — sales, marketing, customer success, product, leadership. With the middleware in place, production is fast and consistent.
Sales decks Battle cards Buyer guides Use case pages Onboarding docs Email journeys FAQs Marketing pages Feature rundowns Board narratives
How It Works
01
Connect
We plug into your existing systems. Whatever you use — CRM, call recordings, team chat, project management, support tickets — we set up continuous intelligence streams. No new tools to adopt. No migration. We work where your data already lives.
02
Build
We construct the middleware layer. ICP documentation, messaging matrix, competitive intel, win/loss patterns, customer profiles. This is the foundation everything else draws from. It’s structured, versioned, and kept current.
03
Ship
We produce collaterals on cadence. Sales needs a new battle card? Built from the middleware. Marketing needs updated positioning for a campaign? Already structured. New feature launching? The messaging is ready. No more one-off requests that take weeks.
04
Compound
The intelligence layer gets richer over time. Every call recorded, every deal closed, every competitor move tracked — it all feeds back in. The longer North runs, the sharper the output. This is infrastructure, not a project.
The Shift

Product marketing used to be entirely manual. With AI agents handling the consumption and structuring, the PMM’s job changes fundamentally.

Before
  • Manually reading call transcripts, one by one
  • Maintaining spreadsheets of competitive intel
  • Building ICP docs from memory and gut feel
  • Creating each sales deck from scratch
  • Messaging that drifts across teams and channels
  • 80% gathering, 20% creating
With North
  • AI agents read and tag every call continuously
  • Competitive intel auto-updates from live sources
  • ICP docs built from real data across all systems
  • Collaterals generated from structured middleware
  • One source of truth for positioning and messaging
  • 20% reviewing, 80% shipping
Who It’s For

North is for B2B software companies that have the data but not the system. You have sales calls, CRM records, team conversations, and customer feedback — but no structured way to turn it into marketing intelligence.

Minimum
At least one marketing team member. You don’t need a PMM team — you need one person who cares about positioning, messaging, and how the market sees your product.
Stage
Series A to Series C. Enough customer conversations and deal data to be useful. Enough product complexity to need structured intelligence.
Signal
Your founder is still doing positioning in a Google Doc. Your sales team makes their own decks. Your messaging drifts every quarter. You know the intelligence is somewhere in your systems — you just can’t access it.
Not For
Pre-product companies. Companies looking for a content agency. Teams that think product marketing is blog posts. Companies with a mature PMM team that needs tools, not operators.
