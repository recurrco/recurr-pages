What Pravar Does

Enterprise AI transformation, not experimentation.

Pravar moves enterprises from AI pilots to AI-native operations. Not a consulting deck. Not a software license. A forward-deployed AI function — platform, people, redesigned workflows — embedded until the work runs on its own.

Why now
Every board is asking the same question: we have spent on AI, nothing has changed. PwC 2026: 56% of companies report zero financial benefit. Half of CEOs tie their job security to getting it right. The budgets exist. The methodology does not. That is the gap.
The Problem

The chasm is not technical.

The technology works. The chasm is organizational — companies cannot rewire how they operate, decide, and deploy talent around AI capabilities.

88%
Experimenting
Pilots. Tools. AI teams. Dashboards for the board. No measurable P&L impact.
74%struggle to move past proof of concept (BCG)
42%have abandoned most AI initiatives (S&P Global)
56%report zero financial benefit (PwC 2026)
Adoption Chasm
5.5%
Transformed
Platform in place, workflows redesigned, talent embedded. AI changes how the company operates. Measurable P&L impact.
1.5×revenue growth vs peers (BCG)
1.6×shareholder returns vs peers (BCG)
+4ppprofit margin (PwC)

The 82.5% in the middle are stuck. The gap widens by quarter — every month in pilot purgatory hands the other side more cost and speed advantage.

The Solution

Three bridges, deployed together.

Platform without workflows is infrastructure no one uses. Talent without a platform has nothing to work with. Run them in sequence and you get pilots; in parallel, you get transformation.

[01]Bridge One
Enterprise AI Platform
Centralized, secure, enterprise-grade. Not a vendor's SaaS — an internal platform built for the org's data, workflows, and regulatory context. Turns isolated experiments into a capability.
Infrastructure that makes scale possible.
[02]Bridge Two
Workflow Redesign
The highest-value workflows rebuilt around AI capabilities. Not "AI-assisted" old processes. New processes that exist only because of AI. 70% of the value lives here.
BCG: 70% of AI value = people + process.
[03]Bridge Three
Talent Injection
AI-native operators embedded next to analysts and decision-makers. Not in an innovation lab. Not in IT. They keep the transformation running after we leave.
What makes the transformation durable.
Year One · The Audience

One boardroom.

Year one is one boardroom — the one where AI impact lands directly in financial figures and the ROI is hard to argue with.

Profile
Boards at firms with $10M+ / ₹50Cr+ revenue.
Half of CEOs tie job security to AI. Leaders are more anxious (43%) than their teams (34%).
Sector
Indian financial services and PE.
Not the biggest market. The clearest one. Financial analysis is the core competency, so every deployment shows up in the P&L. Easy to measure. Easy to publish.
Stakeholder
Where financial analysis is the core competency.
Investment analysis, risk modelling, portfolio management, compliance. Knowledge-intensive, structurally repetitive, P&L-tied. One validated case: ~200 analysts avoided.
Why this works
Compound learning before expansion.
Tight enough to build a repeatable playbook. Large enough to sustain a multi-year practice. Productize from there.

Two assets. Everything else is noise.

The distribution playbook
01
Conferences & Speaking
Show up where the boardroom shows up. Not tech conferences — financial services summits, PE conclaves, CXO forums. Panels, not booths. Goal: 2–3 marquee stages in six months. Ayon's BCG pedigree opens every door.
02
Press & Media
One real interview, not a press blast. A journalist who covers enterprise tech or financial services, where Ayon can name the chasm. ET, Mint, or HBR. The story is the problem, not the company.
03
Publishing
Place in McKinsey Digital, BCG Henderson Institute, HBR.org — the rooms Ayon already knows. In parallel, ship consistently on the Pravar site. The site is a body of work, not a brochure.

Track 02 · Customer Success
The hard truth

01
First three customers from the network

Launch · A Milestone, Not a Marketing Activity
Launch as soon as possible. Not the day the world changes. The day the clock starts.

The problem statement leads. Not "we are a new company" — "88% of enterprises are stuck in pilot purgatory; here is why, and here is the way across."

Before launch
Website + essays
A website that is a body of work, not a brochure. The Adoption Chasm essay plus 2–3 supporting pieces. Live before anything else. This is what a journalist reads. This is what a board member finds when they Google you.
Launch day
Journalist + press release
One journalist, one real interview where Ayon names the chasm. Press release on the same day for baseline coverage. The story is the problem. Aspiration: print in ET or Mint.
Week 1–4
Activate GTM tracks
Both tracks start running. Track 1: speaking applications, publishing pipeline, LinkedIn. Track 2: first three warm intros. The launch gave you something to point to. Use it.
Month 2–6
Compound
First case study ships. Publishing cadence holds. Speaking lands. Each asset reinforces the next. The Adoption Chasm becomes the lens your market uses. You own the category.
Total launch budget
Under ₹10 lakhs. No event, no splash, no PR agency. What compounds is what happens after — consistent execution on both tracks. The launch is a starting gun, not a finish line.

Let's build this.

Three deliverables, in sequence. A deck that compresses the Adoption Chasm to ten slides. A landing page that uses the same sharpened language — the body of work starts there. A founding essay that names the problem and makes Pravar the citation.
