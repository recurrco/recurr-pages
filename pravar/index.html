Strategic Note for Pravar

88% of enterprises are using AI. Only 5.5% are generating value.

The gap between AI experimentation and AI transformation is structural, widening, and unclaimed as a category. This note is a framework for Pravar’s first year — positioning, GTM, and the sequence of bets that get you from stealth to category leader.

What Pravar Does

Enterprise AI transformation, not experimentation.

Pravar helps large enterprises cross from AI pilots to AI-native operations. Not a consulting firm that delivers strategy decks. Not a tool vendor that sells software. Pravar is a forward-deployed AI function — platform, people, and redesigned workflows — embedded inside the enterprise until the transformation is self-sustaining.

Why now
Every board in the country is having the same conversation behind closed doors: "We know AI changes everything. We’ve spent money. Nothing has fundamentally changed." PwC 2026 says 56% of companies are getting zero financial benefit from AI. Half of CEOs believe their job depends on getting this right. The anxiety is real, the budgets are allocated, the methodology doesn’t exist. That’s the gap Pravar walks into.
The Problem

The Adoption Chasm is the structural gap between AI experimentation and AI transformation. It is not a technology problem. The technology works. It is an organizational failure — a fundamental inability to rewire how a company operates, decides, and deploys talent around AI capabilities.

88%
Experimenting
Running pilots, buying tools, hiring "AI teams", presenting dashboards to the board. No measurable business impact.
74% struggle to move beyond proofs of concept (BCG)
42% abandoned most AI initiatives (S&P Global)
56% getting zero financial benefit (PwC 2026)
THE ADOPTION CHASM
5.5%
Transformed
AI has changed how they operate. Platform established, workflows redesigned, talent embedded. Measurable P&L impact.
1.5x higher revenue growth vs peers (BCG)
1.6x greater shareholder returns (BCG)
+4pp higher profit margins (PwC)

The 82.5% in between are trapped. The chasm doesn’t close on its own — it widens. Every quarter in pilot purgatory, the leaders on the other side gain compounding advantages in cost, speed, and capability.

The Widening Gap

Three simultaneous failures — no platform, legacy workflows, no embedded talent — create organizational scar tissue that makes each attempt harder than the last.

The Solution

Pravar is a forward-deployed AI function. Not a consulting engagement. Not a tool. Three bridges, deployed simultaneously, because they are interdependent. A platform without redesigned workflows is infrastructure nobody uses. Talent without a platform has nothing to work with. All three, orchestrated together.

Year One — The Audience

Not every enterprise. Not yet. Year one is about a very specific boardroom — one where AI transformation impact shows up directly in financial figures, making ROI undeniable.

Profile
An anxious boardroom at a firm with $10M+ / ₹50Cr+ revenue
Boards living in fear and uncertainty. Can’t say what they’re really thinking. Half of CEOs believe job security depends on getting AI right. Leaders more anxious (43%) than their own teams (34%).
Sector
Indian Financial Services firms and PEs
Not because it’s the biggest market. Because financial analysis is the core competency — every deployment impact shows up directly in financial figures. Easy to gauge ROI. Easy to build case studies.
Stakeholder
Where financial analysis is the core competency
Investment analysis, risk modelling, portfolio management, compliance. Workflows that are knowledge-intensive, repetitive at the structural level, and directly tied to P&L. One validated case: avoided hiring ~200 investment analysts.
Why this works
Compound learning in one domain before expanding
Every engagement deepens the methodology. Financial services is a tight enough vertical to build repeatable playbooks, large enough to sustain a multi-year practice. Progressive productization from here.
The GTM Split

Pravar has exactly two marketable assets in year one. Everything else is a distraction. The job is to make both assets compound, and to shift weight from Track 1 to Track 2 as fast as possible.

Track 01
The Founders
Ayon and Ranjan are the product. Their credibility, their thinking, their ability to hold the uncomfortable truth about AI transformation in the boardroom. This is the primary asset today — and the primary constraint. Every minute of founder time is finite. This track needs to be amplified, not expanded.
Credibility Network Thought leadership Speaking
Track 02
Customer Success
This track doesn’t exist yet. And that’s the most urgent problem. The first 3 customers need to come from the founders’ network — shortest path to success. But they must be closed with a mindset of shipping customer stories and collaterals at the earliest. Customer success is the second most marketable asset and is needed ASAP to reduce pressure on Track 1 and strengthen it instead.
Case studies Testimonials ROI proof Referenceable logos

The distribution playbook:

Conferences & Speaking +
Show up everywhere the anxious boardroom shows up. Not tech conferences — financial services leadership summits, PE conclaves, CXO forums. The goal is panel seats, not booths. Ayon’s BCG pedigree opens every door. The ask: get on 2-3 marquee stages in the first 6 months.
Press & Media +
One well-placed interview with a journalist who covers enterprise tech or financial services. Not a press release blast. A genuine conversation where Ayon can articulate the Adoption Chasm thesis. ET, Mint, or a global outlet like HBR. The story isn’t “new company launches” — it’s “here’s why 88% of enterprises are stuck.”
Publishing +
Publish on platforms that carry weight in the boardroom: McKinsey Digital, BCG Henderson Institute, HBR.org. Ayon’s former affiliations make this possible. Simultaneously, consistently high-quality publishing on the Pravar website itself — this is the long game. The website becomes a body of work that demonstrates the thinking, not just describes it.
The Thesis as Content +
The “Adoption Chasm” framework is the content strategy. Own this term. A founding essay under Ayon and Ranjan’s names that defines the problem, gives it a name, and makes Pravar the citation. Every subsequent piece of content — talks, interviews, articles — reinforces this frame. The first person to name a problem owns it.
Track 02 — Customer Success
The hard truth
This track does not exist yet. And it is the single biggest bottleneck to Pravar’s growth. Without referenceable customer stories, every sale depends on founder credibility alone. That doesn’t scale.
01
First 3 customers from the network
Not inbound. Not marketing. The fastest path to a referenceable customer is a warm introduction from Ayon or Ranjan’s network. Financial services firms or PEs where someone owes them a meeting. The bar is low: one real engagement, not a pilot.
02
Ship the story, not just the work
Every engagement must be designed for storytelling from day one. The deliverable is transformation AND a case study. Bake documentation, before/after metrics, and permission-to-share into the SOW. If you wait until after the engagement to ask, it’s too late.
03
Reduce pressure on Track 1
Today, every sale requires a founder in the room. Customer stories change that equation. A case study that says “we avoided hiring 200 analysts” does more selling than any founder pitch. The faster Track 2 spins up, the less Track 1 has to carry alone.
Launch — A Milestone, Not a Marketing Activity
Launch should happen ASAP. It is the line from which you start actively marketing on your GTM. Not the day the world changes — the day the clock starts.

The problem statement should lead all messaging. Not “we’re a new company” but “88% of enterprises are stuck in AI pilot purgatory — here’s why, and here’s how to cross.”

Before Launch
Website + Essays
A website that is a body of work, not a brochure. The Adoption Chasm founding essay. 2-3 supporting pieces that demonstrate the thinking. Published and live before anything else happens. This is what a journalist reads before agreeing to cover you. This is what a board member sees when they Google you.
Launch Day
Journalist + Press Release
Engage one journalist you know well. Not a press blast. A genuine interview where Ayon articulates the Adoption Chasm. Push a press release on the same day — wires pick it up, it creates a baseline of coverage. The story is the problem, not the company. The maximum aspiration from this spectrum: print coverage in ET or Mint.
Week 1–4
Activate GTM Tracks
Both tracks start running. Track 1: speaking applications, publishing pipeline, LinkedIn presence. Track 2: first 3 warm intros from network. The launch gave you something to point to. Now you use it.
Month 2–6
Compound
First customer story ships. Publishing cadence established. Speaking engagements land. Each asset reinforces the next. The Adoption Chasm becomes the lens through which your market thinks about the problem. You own the category.
Total launch budget
Under ₹10 lakhs. No event, no splash, no PR agency. What happens after launch day — consistent, high-quality execution on both tracks — is what compounds. The launch is a starting gun, not a finish line.

Let’s build this.

Three deliverables, in sequence: a deck that sharpens the Adoption Chasm into 10 slides (forces brevity, crystallizes the pitch), a landing page that uses the sharpened language (the body of work starts here), and the founding essay (the thought leadership piece that names the problem and makes Pravar the citation).
