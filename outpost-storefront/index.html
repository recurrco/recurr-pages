Glass Storefront — What Goes On It

  • Full vinyl wrap — MOO logo massive on left, DJ bunny mascot vinyl cutout on right
  • Neon strip along top edge of glass — sky blue LED, visible from street at night
  • Menu highlight cards — frosted vinyl panels at bottom showing 3 bestsellers with prices
  • QR code — bottom right, links to Swiggy/ordering page
  • Interior visible through clear glass between vinyl elements — barstools, kitchen activity create life
  • Frosted band at bottom 1ft — hides under-counter clutter, adds premium feel

Entrance Door — Routing

  • Blue header panel — MOO branding at top of door
  • Split signage — delivery partners directed one way, walk-ins another
  • Floor markers inside — colored tape/paint continuing the routing
  • Door pull handle branded with MOO wordmark

Design Decisions

Why Dark Glass

The glass is already dark/tinted (visible in photo). White vinyl + sky blue neon will pop against it. Don't fight the darkness — use it as a canvas. The blue becomes the hero.

Mascot Placement

DJ Bunny at eye level on the right third of the glass. Large enough to be recognizable from across the street. Vinyl cutout (not printed on background) — so it "floats" on the glass.

Audience Separation

Delivery riders need clear, instant wayfinding — they're in a hurry. Color-coded door panels + floor markers. Walk-in/pickup gets a different entry flow so riders don't block the counter.

Menu as Marketing

3 bestseller cards at bottom of glass = impulse awareness for foot traffic. Frosted vinyl cards feel premium, not like a restaurant menu board stuck on glass.

Night Visibility

The neon LED strip along the top edge makes the storefront glow blue at night. Combined with interior lighting visible through clear glass sections, it pulls attention even after dark.

What This Isn't

Not a full opaque wrap — that kills the glass advantage. You can still see inside (barstools, activity, warmth). The vinyl is strategic: branding on top, menu at bottom, clear in the middle.
