The real problem

For a decade, content marketing has been an enormous, ambiguous pile of work. What to make. Where to put it. Blog or Reddit or LinkedIn or video. All of it a bet on getting found when your buyer goes looking.

You pour months into the bet — and find out whether you bet right long after the money's gone.

The pain was never the doing.

It was not knowing what to do — and learning, too late, that you did the wrong thing.
What changed

The intelligence was always there. Reading all of it wasn't.

Every place your buyer searches, asks, compares, and decides has been sitting on the open internet the whole time. It was never missing. It was just impossible for a person to read all of it and turn it into "do these ten things."

That's the part that's now possible. Not "AI changes everything." Just: the distillation that couldn't be done, now can — and it gets sharper every week, because the system learns from what actually worked.
