Why we're opening up positioning — now.

Everything we ship this quarter is downstream of one decision we haven't made yet. This section makes the case for why that decision can't wait.

Between now and September, four things ship — and every one of them is built on top of the positioning.

1.2 · The Stakes03 / 12

We either define a category, or compete inside a shrinking one.

Three forces make the choice urgent — and they all point the same way.
The category

Reporting is commoditizing

Supermetrics, Funnel, Adverity + internal BI all race on connectors and price. Positioned as better reporting, we compete on features in a frame that's getting cheaper.

The buyer

Split down the middle

Marketing buys; the data team deploys and gates. Generic positioning talks to everyone and lands with no one — and deals stall at the committee.

The moment

AI is redrawing "analytics"

LLMs are actively re-defining what analytics means. There's a narrow window to claim a frame before the category resettles around someone else's.

If we act

We position

We name the frame, own a category over time, and everything downstream inherits a clear story.

If we wait

We get positioned

The market — and competitors — define us as "another reporting tool," and we spend the quarter fighting on price and feature parity.

1.3 · The Payoff04 / 12

What a locked position actually buys.

Not a branding nicety — a set of concrete, measurable outcomes.
Coherence → lower CAC
One message across site, content, ads and sales — instead of five teams saying five things.
A category we own
We stop competing on feature-parity against commodity tools and set our own frame.
Qualification
The right message attracts complex, high-velocity accounts (our ICP) and repels cheap-tool shoppers — close-rate and CAC, not just brand.
Pricing power
Escape the "reporting tool" price anchor and price to the value we deliver.
A spine for the pipeline goal
Paid + inbound only convert if the message qualifies the right buyer. Positioning is that filter.
The one-liner
The message is the multiplier on everything else we're about to spend behind. Lock it first.
Section 02 · How We Research It05 / 12
02

We're not guessing our way to a position.

Positioning is a decision we make against evidence, then pressure-test on real buyers before we spend a rupee behind it.
2.1 · The Evidence06 / 12

Four sources, one triangulation.

Each source answers a different question about who we are to the buyer. Together they converge.
Source 01Data-leader discovery calls
We run demos as research and watch the language sharp buyers reach for unprompted. Piyush (Twilio) said "is this Amplitude for marketing?" on his own — validating the category instinct — then surfaced change-velocity as the real ICP filter. 2–3 more scheduled.
Source 02Support-ticket mining
~4,900 tickets analysed. What customers actually hire us for: "the one number I can trust across every channel." Freshness and reconciliation drive the load — that's the job.
Source 03Competitive + search intelligence
What category language has oxygen vs. dead air. Finding: coined category terms — nobody searches them. The buyer's own words beat our invented ones.
Source 04Win / loss signal
Where deals die: the buying committee. Marketing buys, the data team deploys and gates — the message has to clear both, or it stalls at VP/CFO.
2.2 · The Method07 / 12

The discipline.

Three rules keep this rigorous rather than a creative whim.
01
Buyer's words > our words
We anchor on validated language from real buyers — not internal jargon or a coined label.
02
Test before we spend
The working position gets pressure-tested on the next discovery calls before it hits the homepage or paid.
03
Lock once, inherit everywhere
One decision → site, content, ads and sales all ladder to it. No re-litigating downstream.
Framed correctly, locking positioning isn't a delay — it's the move that de-risks the entire H2 spend.
Section 03 · The Working Position08 / 12

Where we've landed — the position we're most confident in right now.

Working, not final. Built to be pressure-tested with you on this call.
3.1 · The Line09 / 12
Marketing changed. Your reporting didn't.
We power data engineering for the world's fastest marketing teams.

Why this line wins

  • Resolves the split buyer. "Data engineering" earns credibility with the data team that deploys; "for marketing teams" keeps the marketer the hero and beneficiary.
  • Escapes the commodity frame. We stop competing as "better reporting" — a shrinking, price-driven category — and reframe as infrastructure.
  • Category-defining, not category-claiming. It's a POV we can own over time, not a coined label nobody searches.
3.2 · The Messaging House10 / 12

One message, built for the whole committee.

The position isn't just a tagline — it's a coherent house that solves the committee problem deals keep dying on.
Hero · the marketer

"Reporting that steers marketing spend at speed and scale."

The one number you can trust — always fresh, always reconciled, across every channel.

Bridge · the data team

"We land governed marketing data in your warehouse."

We're not another silo you babysit. This is the line that gets marketers past VP/CFO.

The job — from 4,900 tickets
One trustworthy number across every channel. Freshness + reconciliation is what they actually hire us for.
Two products, staged
Attribution today (the big door) → MM / incrementality as a consumption-priced data product = Phase 2, the new TAM. Teed up, not forced open now.
3.3 · The Ask11 / 12

Ratify these and the messaging house gets written this week — the homepage and content engine stop guessing.

Keep "Marketing Data Plane" as a thought-leadership POV — not the product label. The product speaks the buyer's language; the category emerges from the POV.

Any hard objection to this as the through-line for site + content + outbound?
3
