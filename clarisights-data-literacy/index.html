The Thesis

No one owns the standard for paid marketing data. Marketers managing $500K+/month across five or more channels are making up naming conventions, reinventing report structures, and silently losing trust in their own numbers. Every company solves this from scratch. Every agency has a different system. There is no shared language.

Clarisights sees more cross-platform data than almost anyone. The nomenclature issues, the transformation failures, the "why don't my numbers match" conversations that happen in every onboarding, every QBR, every renewal risk call. This is institutional knowledge that has never been externalized.

This program turns that knowledge into the industry's reference material. Three layers, twelve weeks, one standard.

Guiding Principles
Specificity over scale.
No "Top 10 Marketing KPIs" listicles. Every page answers a specific question a specific marketer has at a specific moment. "What does ROAS mean in DV360 and why is it different from Google Ads ROAS?" That level of specificity.
Nomenclature is not a footnote.
Data naming, field taxonomy, API conventions — this is the core differentiator. Most content programs treat this as boring reference material. We treat it as the central thesis: if your naming is broken, your data is broken, and no tool can save you.
Teach the craft, not the tool.
Every piece of content should be useful even if the reader never uses Clarisights. That's what builds trust. The product sells itself when the reader realizes the standard they just learned is built into the platform.
Grounded in real operations, not theory.
Every naming convention, every report template, every KPI framework comes from real enterprise teams running real money through real platforms. CS conversations, not marketing brainstorms, are the source material.
The graph, not the page.
Individual pages capture traffic. The interconnected graph builds authority. Every Layer 1 page links to Layer 2 context. Every Layer 2 playbook references Layer 1 definitions. Every Layer 3 piece drives back to both. The system compounds.
