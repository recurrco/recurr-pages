How to read them

What each direction is going for

V1 · Brown & Gold

Heritage, parcel-brown prestige

  • Warmest, most "established institution" of the three
  • Gold pops hard against deep brown
  • Team feedback: the golden-brown combo felt heavy / not quite Quad
  • Schools: Teal · Crimson · Violet (most varied, least unified)
V2 · Crimson

Restrained institutional red

  • Promotes red to the foundation; gold becomes the thread
  • Deliberately muted spectrum — "warm, refined, never neon"
  • Schools are quiet siblings (Sapphire · Burgundy · Plum)
  • Premium and safe; can read as low-energy next to V3
V3 · Crimson & Press (new)

Timeless face, editorial energy

  • Foundation is strict crimson / black / white / gold
  • Adds a bright "Press" spectrum for the contemporary layer
  • Schools lead with brights (Cobalt · Tangerine · Magenta)
  • Most range: serious for parents, vivid for students

Want a fourth that blends two of these — say V2's restraint with V3's bright accents, or V3's foundation with cooler school colours? Flag it and we'll mock it.
